Moments and joint nonvanishing of symplectic $L$-functions

Valentin Blomer, Soumya Das

L-functions are complex-analytic objects encoding deep arithmetic information about automorphic forms, and knowing they don't vanish at specific points has major number-theoretic consequences. This paper computes asymptotic formulas for moments — averaged products — of two L-functions attached to degree-two Siegel cusp forms, then uses these to prove both functions are simultaneously nonzero for many forms. This addresses a core challenge in analytic number theory: establishing nonvanishing results where neither function alone is sufficient.

math.NT
Original abstract

We compute an asymptotic formula for a moment involving the spinor and the standard $L$-functions for holomorphic Siegel cusp forms of degree two and large weight $k$. Applications include simultaneous non-vanishing statements and lower bounds for second moments.

Mellin transforms, transfinite diameter and rational approximations of integrals

Francis Brown

Can we prove that specific integrals arising in physics and number theory are irrational? This paper develops a higher-dimensional irrationality criterion for 'periods'—numbers expressible as Mellin transforms (a generalization of Fourier transforms)—framed as an upper bound on the multivariate transfinite diameter, a measure of how spread out a set is relative to polynomial approximations. The approach offers a systematic tool for attacking open irrationality questions about classical constants.

math.NT
Original abstract

We establish a higher-dimensional irrationality criterion for periods which are presented as Mellin integrals depending on many parameters. The criterion is stated as an upper bound on the multi-variate transfinite diameter of the image of the domain of integration under the Mellin arguments. Most of the paper is devoted to studying notions of transfinite diameter relative to very general multivariate Vandermonde matrices. As a proof of principle, we illustrate how this approach works with detailed computations in the case of a 5-parameter family of integrals for $ζ(2)$ on $\mathcal{M}_{0,5}$, the moduli space of curves of genus 0 with 5 marked points. This yields a `higher-dimensional' proof of the irrationality of $ζ(2)$, based on an upper bound for a certain kind of transfinite diameter associated to $\mathcal{M}_{0,5}$.

p-adic elliptic polylogarithms and cubic Chabauty

Jennifer S. Balakrishnan, Francesca Bianchi, Netan Dogra

The Chabauty-Kim method finds integer points on curves by locating zeros of p-adic functions — numbers built from prime-based analogs of logarithms. This paper gives an explicit formula for a depth-3 version of these functions for elliptic curves of rank one, going one level deeper than previously known. This advances a program aimed at provably computing all rational points on curves, a central goal in number theory.

math.NT math.AG
Original abstract

The Chabauty--Coleman--Kim method, under favourable circumstances, describes the set of integral points of a hyperelliptic curve inside the $p$-adic zeroes of certain transcendental functions. For an elliptic curve of Mordell--Weil rank one, the Chabauty--Coleman--Kim set in depth 2 is given by the zeroes of a (finite union of) quadratic polynomial(s) in the $p$-adic logarithm of the elliptic curve and the local $p$-adic height at $p$. Here, we give an explicit formula for a finite set containing the Chabauty--Coleman--Kim set in depth 3 for an elliptic curve of rank at most 2 under an assumption on non-vanishing of a special value of a $p$-adic $L$-function. The finite set is given by the zeroes of a polynomial in $p$-adic elliptic polylogarithms. We use these formulas to verify new instances of Kim's conjecture.

On the singularities of differential equations satisfied by $E$-functions

Stéphane Fischler, Tanguy Rivoal

E-functions are power series with algebraically controlled coefficients that satisfy linear differential equations; a key question is whether their special values can be represented by E-functions whose differential equations are 'nice' at a given point. This paper proves you can always avoid a singularity at 1, but not at 0 for Bessel function values—answering an analogue of a question André posed for related G-functions. It clarifies a subtle structural asymmetry in how these special functions encode arithmetic information.

math.NT
Original abstract

Let $ξ$ be a value, at an algebraic point, of a Siegel $E$-function. As a special case of a very general interpolation result, we prove that there exists an $E$-function $f$ such that $f(1)=ξ$, and such that 1 is not a singularity of the minimal differential equation satisfied by $f$. We prove that the same property does not hold at the point $0$, when $ξ$ is the value at a non-zero algebraic number of the Bessel function. This answers an analogue of a question asked by Yves Andr{é} for $G$-functions.

Representations of binary quadratic forms by quaternary quadratic forms

Wooyeon Kim, Andreas Wieser, Pengyu Yang

Can you tell whether a binary quadratic form (a degree-2 polynomial in 2 variables) is represented by a quaternary quadratic form (degree-2 in 4 variables) just by checking locally at every prime? This paper proves yes—a local-global principle holds for primitive representations—using Linnik's ergodic method and a deep measure rigidity result for diagonal group actions. This resolves a natural higher-dimensional generalization of classical representation problems in number theory.

math.NT math.DS
Original abstract

We prove a local-global principle for primitive representations of binary quadratic forms by quaternary quadratic forms. Our method is a variant of Linnik's ergodic method showing density for certain homogenous toral sets. The central ingredient is a measure classification result of Einsiedler and Lindenstrauss for actions of rank two diagonalizable groups on quotients of products of $\mathrm{SL}_2$. This rigidity result together with an application of the Siegel mass formula reduces the density problem to a counting problem on a certain affine variety. We solve that counting problem using the determinant method of Bombieri-Pila and Heath-Brown.

Division algorithms for norm-Euclidean imaginary quadratic fields

François Morain

Norm-Euclidean imaginary quadratic fields are number systems — like the Gaussian integers — where a Euclidean division algorithm exists using the field's norm. There are only finitely many such fields, and this paper provides explicit, optimized division algorithms for each one that always find remainders strictly within the field's best-possible distance bound, the Euclidean minimum.

math.NT
Original abstract

The list of norm-Euclidean imaginary quadratic fields is known and finite. For each known case, we give a division algorithm that finds a remainder at distance less than the Euclidean minimum of the field.

Quartic Rational Diophantine Quadruples and the Euler Surface

Alen Andrašek, Matija Kazalicki, Domagoj Vlah

A Diophantine quadruple is a set of four rationals where every pairwise product plus 1 is a perfect square; this paper asks the same question with fourth powers instead. The authors construct infinitely many such 'quartic rational Diophantine quadruples'—the first known examples—by connecting the problem to rational points on the Euler surface X⁴+Y⁴=Z⁴+W⁴, a classical object in number theory. This resolves a natural open question about higher-power variants of a centuries-old problem.

math.NT
Original abstract

We prove that there exist infinitely many quartic rational Diophantine quadruples, that is, sets of four pairwise distinct nonzero rational numbers whose pairwise products increased by 1 are fourth powers in Q. To the best of our knowledge, no examples of such quadruples were previously known. Our construction is motivated by computer experiments and leads naturally to the classical Euler surface E:X^4+Y^4=Z^4+W^4. We show that every rational point on a suitable Zariski-open subset of E yields a quartic rational Diophantine quadruple, thereby obtaining a rational map from the Euler surface to the parameter space of quartic quadruples. In particular, Euler's classical parametrization produces the first explicit infinite family of quartic rational Diophantine quadruples. We also explain that the same mechanism extends to arbitrary exponents k>1, with the Euler surface replaced by the Fermat--Euler surface E_k:X^k+Y^k=Z^k+W^k. For even k, every rational point on a suitable open subset of E_k gives rise to a kth power rational Diophantine quadruple, while for odd k one obtains such quadruples on the locus where W/Z is a square.

Beyond the square-root barrier: cubic forms of Perazzo type

Tim Browning, Ritabrata Munshi, Victor Y. Wang

The circle method is a powerful analytic technique for counting rational solutions to polynomial equations, but it typically struggles beyond a 'square-root barrier' limiting its accuracy. This paper pushes past that barrier for a specific cubic fourfold (a degree-3 hypersurface in 5-dimensional space) of Perazzo type. This matters because breaking this barrier opens new territory for understanding rational points on higher-dimensional varieties where standard tools fall short.

math.NT
Original abstract

We show how the circle method can be used to study rational points on a certain cubic fourfold, going beyond the square-root barrier.

Algebraic numbers and Fourier analysis: Salem's third problem

Khoa D. Nguyen

Salem's 1963 problem asked whether products of Fourier transforms of Bernoulli convolutions — probability measures built from random sums of powers of a fixed number — vanish at infinity. This paper solves that third problem from Salem's famous list. The result connects algebraic number theory to harmonic analysis, closing a 60-year-old question while two of Salem's other problems remain completely open.

math.NT
Original abstract

In 1963, Raphaël Salem concluded his highly influential book ``Algebraic Numbers and Fourier Analysis'' with a list of four unsolved problems. The first two problems remain wide open while the last problem on the absolute continuity of Bernoulli convolutions has seen significant progress over the years including recent results by Shmerkin and Varjú. In this paper, we solve the third problem concerning the vanishing at infinity of the product of Fourier transforms of Bernoulli convolutions each of which does not vanish at infinity. Our solution uses tools in diophantine approximation such as the theory of Weil heights and Lang's general formulation of Roth's theorem.

Duality Between Prime Factors and The Prime Number Theorem For Arithmetic Progressions -- Higher Order Dualities

Krishnaswami Alladi, Sroyon Sengupta

Can a symmetry between the largest and smallest prime factors of integers yield new number-theoretic results? This paper develops 'higher-order dualities' linking the k-th largest and k-th smallest prime factors of an integer, using the Möbius function—a central tool encoding multiplicative structure—and the Prime Number Theorem for arithmetic progressions. These dualities produce new asymptotic formulas, advancing a program started in 1977 connecting prime factor statistics to classical analytic number theory.

math.NT
Original abstract

In 1977, the first author observed a duality between the largest and smallest prime factors of integers, and established as a consequence some new results on the Möbius function $μ(n)$ using the Prime Number Theorem for Arithmetic Progressions. In that 1977 paper, higher order dualities were observed involving the $k$-th largest and $k$-th smallest prime factors, facilitated by the Möbius function and $ω(n)^{k-1}$, where $ω(n)$ is the number of distinct prime factors on $n$. In 2024, the first author and Jason Johnson proved new results involving $μ(n)$ and $ω(n)$, by exploiting the second order duality identity of Alladi (1977). We establish here extensions to all higher orders $k$, the results of Alladi (1977) and of Alladi-Johnson (2024), by utilizing the $k$-th order duality in Alladi's 1977 paper. First, we show that for each $k\geq 2$, $$ \sum_{n=2}^{\infty} \frac{μ(n)ω(n)^{k}}{n} =0, $$ where $μ(n)$ is the Möbius Function and $ω(n)$ counts the number of distinct prime factors of $n$. Further, using the General Duality Identity and the Prime Number Theorem of Arithmetic Progressions, we prove that for integers $j,\ell$ satisfying $1 \leq j \leq \ell$ and $(j,\ell)=1$ $$ \sum_{\substack{n=2 \\ p_1(n) \equiv j\;(mod\;\ell)}}^{\infty} \frac{μ(n)ω(n)^{k-1}}{n}=0, \nonumber $$ for every $k \geq 3$; this result for $k=1$ is due to Alladi (1977) and for $k=2$ due to Alladi-Johnson (2024). We also recast this result in the following manner as a density-type theorem: for integers $j,\ell$ satisfying $1 \leq j \leq \ell$ and $(j,\ell)=1$ $$ (-1)^k\sum_{\substack{n=2 \\ p_1(n) \equiv j\;(mod\;\ell)}}^{\infty} \frac{μ(n){ω(n)-1 \choose k-1}}{n}=\frac{1}{\varphi(\ell)}, \nonumber $$ for every $k \geq 3$. All results are established here in quantitative form.

Semistable Reduction Theorem for Overconvergent $F$-isocrystals over Laurent Series Fields

Yuanmin Liu

Can wildly ramified families of p-adic differential equations always be tamed by a finite base change? This paper proves a semistable reduction theorem for overconvergent F-isocrystals—coefficient systems for p-adic cohomology theories—over varieties defined over Laurent series fields. As a payoff, it establishes finite-dimensionality of rigid cohomology with compact support, a key foundational property for p-adic point-counting in arithmetic geometry.

math.NT math.AG
Original abstract

We prove the semistable reduction theorem for $\mathcal{E}^†_K$-valued and $K$-valued overconvergent $F$-isocrystals over $k((t))$-varieties which were introduced by Lazda and Pál. As an application, we prove the finite dimensionality of $\mathcal{E}^†_K$-valued rigid cohomology with compact support.

Resolutions of spaces of crystalline representations and modularity

Robin Bartlett, Bao V. Le Hung, Brandon Levin

Crystalline representations are a well-behaved class of representations of Galois groups (symmetries of number fields) central to modern number theory. This paper constructs a new geometric resolution of their parameter spaces and uses it to prove modularity results — connecting these Galois representations to automorphic forms — for three-dimensional cases, advancing the Langlands program without restricting ramification.

math.NT
Original abstract

We introduce a new partial resolution of crystalline spaces of Galois representations when the gaps in Hodge--Tate weights are smaller than $p$, with no bound on ramification. Furthermore, when $n =3$ in the case of minimal regular weight, we are able to show that the resolution is normal (assuming the ramification index is divisible by 3). Employing base change techniques and further analysis of the resolution, we are able to show that all the components of the crystalline deformation rings are potentially diagonalizable. As a consequence, we deduce automorphy lifting, the weight part of Serre's conjecture, and the Breuil-Mézard conjecture in dimension three for minimal regular weight.

Hodge theory for combinatorial projective bundles

Matt Larson, Ethan Partida

Do the geometric properties governing the shape of algebraic varieties extend to purely combinatorial settings? This paper proves two powerful results from classical geometry — the Hard Lefschetz theorem and Hodge-Riemann relations — for algebraic structures mimicking the cohomology of certain fiber bundles over toric varieties. This resolves new cases of a longstanding conjecture and yields concrete results about numerical invariants (tautological classes) of matroids, combinatorial objects encoding linear independence.

math.AG math.CO
Original abstract

We prove the Hard Lefschetz theorem and Hodge-Riemann relations for certain rings which resemble the cohomology rings of projectivizations of globally generated vector bundles over toric varieties. This proves new cases of the standard conjecture of Hodge type and gives Bloch-Gieseker-type results for tautological classes of matroids.

$E_6$-local systems from cubic threefolds

Thomas Krämer, Daniel Litt, Marco Maculan

Local systems are algebraic analogues of representations that vary continuously over a space, and exceptional Lie groups like E₆ are rare, exotic symmetry groups. This paper constructs infinitely many local systems on moduli spaces of cubic threefolds whose symmetry group is exactly E₆, arising from the geometry of lines on these threefolds. This is surprising because E₆ local systems in geometric settings are very hard to find, and this gives a natural, concrete source of them.

math.AG
Original abstract

We produce infinitely many local systems on (level covers of) the moduli space of smooth cubic threefolds, with algebraic monodromy group equal to the exceptional group $E_6$. These local systems arise in the middle cohomology of abelian étale covers of the Fano scheme parametrizing lines in the universal cubic threefold.

On G-birational rigidity of projective spaces

Ivan Cheltsov, Frederic Mangolte, Constantin Shramov

When can a finite symmetry group of projective space 'lock' it in place, preventing equivariant simplification via birational maps? This paper proves that for each dimension n≥3, only finitely many such 'G-birationally rigid' subgroups exist up to conjugation, and identifies a specific sporadic group making 4-dimensional projective space maximally rigid. G-birational rigidity means no G-equivariant rational map can simplify the space's structure, a key concept in the Mori fiber space program.

math.AG
Original abstract

In this paper, we study finite subgroups $G\subset\mathrm{Aut}(\mathbb{P}^n)$ such that $\mathbb{P}^n$ is $G$-birationally rigid. For each $n\geqslant 3$, we prove that $\mathrm{Aut}(\mathbb{P}^n)$ contains at most finitely many such subgroups up to conjugation. For $n=4$, we prove that $\mathbb{P}^4$ is $G$-birationally superrigid if $G\simeq\mathrm{PSp}_{4}(\mathbf{F}_3)$.

G-birationally rigid cubic threefolds

Ivan Cheltsov, Igor Krylov, Sione Ma'u

A cubic threefold is a degree-3 hypersurface in 4-dimensional projective space, and G-birational rigidity asks whether a symmetry group G prevents any equivariant simplification of the space. This paper fully classifies all pairs (cubic threefold, finite symmetry group) with this rigidity property, including singular cubics. The result closes a significant chapter in equivariant Mori theory by pinpointing exactly which symmetries make these classical varieties irreducible.

math.AG
Original abstract

We classify pairs $(X,G)$ consisting of a (possibly singular) cubic threefold $X\subset\mathbb{P}^4$ and a finite subgroup $G\subset\mathrm{Aut}(X)$ such that $X$ is $G$-birationally rigid, i.e., $X$ is a $G$-Mori fiber space (over a point), and $X$ is not $G$-birational to any $G$-Mori fibre space that is not $G$-biregular to $X$.

Birational geometry of actions on del Pezzo surfaces

Ivan Cheltsov, Yuri Tschinkel, Zhijia Zhang

Del Pezzo surfaces are algebraic surfaces classified by their 'degree,' and birational equivalence asks when two surfaces can be related by rational maps. This paper completes the classification of finite group actions on del Pezzo surfaces up to birational equivalence, settling several open problems including which actions are birationally rigid. It provides a comprehensive dictionary between symmetry groups and the birational geometry of these fundamental surfaces.

math.AG
Original abstract

We complete the classification of regular generically free actions of finite groups on del Pezzo surfaces, up to birational equivalence. As a byproduct, we settle several open problems in equivariant birational geometry, e.g., we classify birationally rigid actions on del Pezzo surfaces.

Higher rank Gelfand-Kapranov-Zelevinsky fans

Rocco Chirivì, Martina Costa Cesari, Xin Fang et al.

GKZ-fans are geometric objects that organize how a set of points in space can be triangulated or subdivided, with deep connections to toric varieties (geometric objects built from combinatorial data). This paper generalizes GKZ-fans to 'higher rank,' capturing richer degeneration data, and uses them to construct structured degenerations of toric varieties into unions of simpler toric pieces. This extends a fundamental tool in combinatorial algebraic geometry to a more powerful, hierarchical framework.

math.AG math.AC math.CO
Original abstract

We define and study the higher rank GKZ-fans of point configurations, where the rank one cases coincide with the usual GKZ-fans. A point in a higher rank GKZ-fan is then used to construct higher rank quasi-valuations to degenerate the toric variety associated to the point configuration flatly to a reduced union of toric varieties. Such a union encodes the polytopal subdivision arising from the point in the higher rank GKZ-fan.

Representability of codimension three cycles

Kalyan Banerjee

Can higher-dimensional geometric cycles be understood through simpler ones on surfaces? This paper develops a framework for 'representability' of codimension-three cycles on fourfolds—geometric objects of dimension one inside a four-dimensional variety—by expressing them in terms of zero-dimensional cycles on surfaces, building a bridge between high-dimensional algebraic geometry and more tractable lower-dimensional invariants.

In this paper, we develop the notion of representability of co-dimension three cycles on a fourfold in terms of zero cycles modulo rational equivalence on surfaces.

Maximally nodal sextic surfaces and linear determinantal representations

Yonghwa Cho

A sextic surface in 3D projective space can have at most 65 singular points (nodes); can such maximally singular surfaces always be expressed as determinants of matrices? This paper proves every such surface admits a symmetric 6×6 matrix of linear forms whose determinant defines it, with the construction passing through a special configuration of 35 nodes and producing an Ulrich sheaf—a particularly well-behaved algebraic object—as a byproduct.

math.AG
Original abstract

We prove that every maximally nodal sextic surface\,(with 65 nodes) $X \subset \mathbb{P}_{\mathbb{C}}^3$ contains a symmetric half-even set of nodes of cardinality 35. It follows that the associated half-quadratic sheaf is the cokernel of a symmetric $6 \times 6$ matrix of linear forms, yielding a linear determinantal representation of $X$. In particular, after a suitable Serre twist, the half-quadratic sheaf is an Ulrich sheaf of rank 1. As an example, we exhibit an explicit $6 \times 6$ matrix of linear forms whose determinant defines the Barth sextic surface.

Non-Abelian p-Curvature and a Non-Abelian Katz's Formula

Michael Barz

P-curvature measures how a differential equation behaves when reduced modulo a prime p, and Katz's formula connects it to classical geometric data (the Kodaira–Spencer map) for a family of algebraic varieties. This paper extends that formula to the 'non-abelian' setting of non-abelian Hodge theory using a modern stacky framework, delivering the more conceptual proof that Lam–Litt had anticipated and strengthening the bridge between characteristic-p geometry and p-adic Hodge theory.

math.AG
Original abstract

Let $k$ be a field of characteristic $p,$ and $f : X \to S$ a smooth proper morphism of smooth $k$-schemes. Katz's formula gives a relationship between the Kodaira--Spencer map of $f,$ and an invariant called the $p$-curvature of the Gauss--Manin connection associated to $f.$ Recently, Lam--Litt proved a variant of Katz's formula in non-abelian Hodge theory, and suggested that it should be possible to give a more conceptual proof of their formula using the stacky approach to $p$-adic Hodge theory. In this article, we realize their suggestion, explaining how the rather concrete phenomena observed by Katz and Lam--Litt can be explained in a conceptual way using sheared de Rham stacks, as developed by Bhatt--Kanaev--Vologodsky--Zhang and Drinfeld (though we prove a slightly different statement than Lam--Litt do). We do not assume the reader has any background in the theory of de Rham stacks.

Border subrank of higher order tensors and algebras

Chia-Yu Chang, Fulvio Gesmundo, Jeroen Zuiddam

Tensor rank measures the computational complexity of operations like matrix multiplication. This paper pins down the 'border subrank' — a refined complexity measure using limits of decompositions — for structure tensors of several algebra families, including tight bounds for iterated matrix multiplication. This matters because subrank controls how efficiently repeated algebraic operations can be parallelized, and exact values are notoriously hard to compute.

math.AG cs.CC math.RA quant-ph
Original abstract

We determine the border subrank of higher order structure tensors of several families of algebras, and in particular obtain the following results. (1) We determine tight bounds on the border subrank of $k$-fold matrix multiplication and $k$-fold upper triangular matrix multiplication for all $k$. (2) We determine the border subrank of the higher order structure tensors of truncated polynomial algebras, null algebras, and apolar algebras of a quadric. (3) We determine the border subrank of the higher order structure tensors of the Lie algebra $\mathfrak{sl}_2$ for all orders. (4) We prove that degeneration of structure tensors of algebras propagates from higher to lower order. Along the way, we investigate which upper bound methods (geometric rank, $G$-stable rank, socle degree) are effective in which settings, and how they relate. Our work extends the results of Strassen (J.~Reine Angew.~Math., 1987, 1991), who determined the asymptotic subrank of these algebras for tensors of order three, in two directions: we determine the border subrank itself rather than its asymptotic version, and we consider higher order structure tensors.

The non-perturbative topological string: from resurgence to wall-crossing of DT invariants

Simon Douaud, Amir-Kian Kashani-Poor

Topological string theory produces formal power series that diverge, and resurgence is a technique that extracts meaningful non-perturbative information from such divergences. This paper shows the algebraic structure governing these divergences — called alien derivatives — is isomorphic to the Kontsevich-Soibelman Lie algebra, directly linking string perturbation theory to wall-crossing phenomena for Donaldson-Thomas invariants, which count geometric objects on manifolds.

hep-th math-ph math.AG
Original abstract

We study the resurgence structure of the topological string partition function, with an emphasis on the Borel analysis of the instanton amplitudes. To this end, we introduce a differential operator that implements the pointed alien derivative when acting on the topological string partition function and its iterated alien derivatives. We show that the algebra of alien derivatives is isomorphic to the Kontsevich-Soibelman Lie algebra, thus establishing a direct link between the resurgence of the topological string and wall-crossing of generalized Donaldson-Thomas invariants. Numerically, we continue the exploration of the Borel plane of the quintic and local $\mathbb{P}^2$. For the latter, we identify Borel singularities due to bound states involving D4-branes, and match the associated Stokes constants to the appropriate Donaldson-Thomas invariants. Finally, we identify the manifestation of a D2-brane decay in the Borel plane, and match to theoretical predictions.

The integral Chow ring of $\mathscr{M}_{0}(\mathbb{P}^r, 2)$

Renzo Cavalieri, Damiano Fulghesu

The Chow ring of a moduli space encodes intersection-theoretic data about geometric objects it parametrizes. This paper gives an explicit three-variable polynomial ring presentation for the integral Chow ring of the moduli stack of degree-2 maps from rational curves to projective space, with the relations across different dimensions encoded by just two rational generating functions — a surprisingly clean combinatorial structure.

math.AG
Original abstract

We compute a presentation for the integral Chow rings of the moduli stacks of degree $2$ maps from smooth rational curves to projective space $\mathbb{P}^r$, as a quotient of a three-variable polynomial ring. The relations as $r$ varies have rich combinatorial structure: all non-trivial relations are encoded by two generating functions which are rational functions.

Generalized algebraic Morse inequalities and Hasse-Schmidt jet differentials

Benoit Cadorel

Green-Griffiths jet differentials are algebraic objects that detect entire curves (images of the complex line) in complex manifolds, central to the Kobayashi hyperbolicity conjecture. This paper gives a fully algebraic proof that manifolds of general type — the most complex algebraic varieties — carry many such differentials, replacing analytic tools with new algebraic Morse inequalities that bound dimensions of cohomology groups.

math.AG
Original abstract

This is a remastered and expanded version of a an earlier preprint of the author, in which we give a fully algebraic proof of an important theorem of Demailly, stating the existence of many Green-Griffiths jet differentials on a complex projective manifold of general type. To this end, we introduce a new algebraic version of the Morse inequalities, which we use in our proof as an algebraic counterpart to Demailly's and Bonavero's holomorphic Morse inequalities. This new version also applies to positive characteristic, giving the existence of Hasse-Schmidt jet differentials for a smooth projective variety of general type over an arbitrary algebraically closed field.

On the Hurwitz existence problem for branched covers of the projective line

Ciro Ciliberto, Andreas Leopold Knutsen, Sara Torelli

When can a map between surfaces exist with prescribed branching behavior? The Hurwitz existence problem asks which 'ramification profiles'—descriptions of how sheets of a covering wrap around branch points—are actually realizable. This paper completely solves the problem for covers of the projective line where every ramification point lies over a distinct branch point, closing a long-standing gap for this natural family of profiles.

Original abstract

We solve the Hurwitz existence problems for branched covers of $\mathbb{P}^1$ in the case where the number of ramification points equals the number of branch points, that is, where all the ramification profiles are of the form $[e,1,\ldots,1]$ with $e \geq 2$.

Gavril Farkas, Alessandro Verra

The moduli space of odd spin curves of genus 9—roughly, the parameter space of genus-9 curves equipped with a specific square root of their canonical bundle—is shown to be unirational, meaning it can be parametrized by rational functions. This is the highest genus where such geometric 'simplicity' is confirmed, achieved by relating these spaces to smaller pointed spin curve moduli spaces via projective bundle structures.

math.AG
Original abstract

We show that the moduli space of odd spin curves of genus 9 is unirational. This is the highest genus for which such a result is known. This is achieved by realizing birationally the moduli space of odd spin curves of genus g<10 as a locally trivial projective bundle over a certain (finite quotient of the) moduli space of n-pointed odd stable spin curves of genus g'<g. Universal compactified Jacobians: cohomological invariance and boundary combinatorics

Rahul Pandharipande, Dan Petersen, Johannes Schmitt et al.

A compactified Jacobian is a moduli space parametrizing line bundles on singular algebraic curves, and different stability conditions can produce different-looking compactifications. Despite this, a theorem implies their cohomology is always the same—a surprising fact. This paper explains the mystery by developing combinatorial tools for the boundary structure and establishing cohomological invariance directly, giving geometric insight into why these seemingly different spaces share identical topological invariants.

math.AG
Original abstract
