Counting Theorems for Algebraic Relations

Gal Binyamini, Noriko Hirata-Kohno, Makoto Kawashima et al.

O-minimal structures provide a tame setting for counting rational or algebraic points on definable sets, generalizing Wilkie's conjecture about sparse intersections. This paper conjectures and partially proves that, outside an 'algebraic part,' intersections with rational algebraic varieties grow polynomially in degree and height, with implications for open problems in algebraic independence theory.

math.NT math.AG math.LO
Original abstract

Let X be a set definable in a sharply o-minimal structure. We consider the problem of counting the number of points where X intersects algebraic varieties V over Q of dimension k < codim X, as a function of T := deg(V) + h(V), where h(V) is the log-height of V. In particular, we conjecture that after removing a suitable "algebraic part", this number grows polynomially in T -- a generalization of Wilkie's conjecture. We show that this full conjecture implies some open problems in algebraic independence theory. We also formulate a weaker conjecture stating that all intersections above are contained in a poly(T) amount of balls of radius e^{-T}. We then consider the case where X (subset of C^n) is a (compact piece of a) trajectory of a polynomial differential equation satisfying a variant of Nesterenko's D-property. Our main theorem is a proof of the weakened conjecture for such curves when k < sqrt(n) - 1.

Gelfand--Kirillov dimension and mod $p$ cohomology for inner forms of $\mathrm{GL}_2$

Andrea Dotto, Bao V. Le Hung

How large, in a precise algebraic sense, are the symmetry eigenspaces appearing in the mod p cohomology of certain arithmetic quotients? This paper computes the Gelfand–Kirillov dimension—a measure of how fast a representation grows—of these spaces for inner forms of GL₂ (groups built from division algebras) over totally real fields. Beyond the new cases, the method also streamlines an existing proof, suggesting a unified framework for mod p Langlands theory.

math.NT math.RT
Original abstract

Under standard assumptions, we compute the GK-dimension of Hecke eigenspaces in the mod $p$ cohomology of an inner form $D^\times$ of $\mathrm{GL}_2$ over a totally real field unramified at $p$, allowing $D$ to be a division algebra at $p$. Our arguments also apply when $D$ is a matrix algebra at $p$, in which case they give a simplified proof of a theorem of Breuil--Herzig--Hu--Morra--Schraen.

On the Number of Prime Factors of Consecutive Integers

Cheuk Fung Lau

How many distinct prime factors can consecutive integers share? This paper shows infinitely many integers n exist where every nearby integer n+k has at most O(log k) distinct prime factors, sharpening a recent bound of O(k). The function ω(n) counts distinct prime factors of n. This logarithmic improvement resolves several longstanding questions posed by Erdős about the arithmetic structure of consecutive integers.

math.NT
Original abstract

We prove that there are infinitely many $n$ such that $ω(n+k) \ll \log k$ for all integers $k \ge 2$. This improves on a result of Tao-Teräväinen (2025), who has $O(k)$ in place of $O(\log k)$. As corollaries, we make progress on a number of questions posed by Erdős. The proof is based on a quantitative refinement of the Tao-Teräväinen probabilistic argument, combining a more efficient sieve procedure with stronger exponential concentration-of-measure estimates. Moreover, we formulate a conjecture on integers with many prime factors based on Cramér-type random models. Assuming this conjecture, the main bound is essentially sharp.

Fourier Dimension in Inhomogeneous Duffin--Schaeffer Conjecture

Bo Tan, Qing-Long Zhou

How well can real numbers be approximated by fractions with denominators from a restricted set, when the target fractions are shifted by some offset? This paper studies the 'Fourier dimension' — a measure of how well a fractal set supports oscillatory functions — of such inhomogeneous approximation sets, extending the classical Duffin–Schaeffer framework. The results quantify the fine structure of these sets in cases where standard measure-theoretic tools give incomplete information.

math.NT
Original abstract

Let \(Q \subseteq \mathbb{N}\) be a subset, and let \(ψ\colon \mathbb{N} \to [0, \tfrac{1}{2})\), \(θ\colon \mathbb{N} \to \mathbb{R}\) be functions. Let \(\{A_q\}\) and \(\{B_q\}\) be sequences of integers such that \(\gcd(A_q, B_q) = 1\) and \(B_q > 0\) for all \(q\). Define \(W_Q^{\ast}(ψ,θ)\) to be the set of \(x \in [0,1]\) for which \[ \left| x - \frac{p + θ(q)}{q} \right| < \frac{ψ(q)}{q} \] holds for infinitely many \((p,q) \in \mathbb{Z} \times Q\) with \(\gcd(B_q p + A_q, q) = 1\). In this paper, we determine the Fourier dimension of \(W_Q^{\ast}(ψ,θ)\). Our result not only recovers the classical theorems of Kaufman and Bluhm (concerning the homogeneous case \(ψ(q) = q^{-τ}\) with \(τ\ge 1\)) and the one-dimensional version of a result by Cai and Hambrook on the inhomogeneous approximable set, but also provides a complete inhomogeneous generalization. Moreover, it gives an affirmative answer to the coprime formulation of the Chen--Xiong conjecture.

A proof of $p$-adic Gross--Zagier theorem via BDP formula

Kâzım Büyükboduk, Peter Neamti

The p-adic Gross–Zagier formula relates derivatives of p-adic L-functions (arithmetic objects encoding number-theoretic data) to heights of special points on elliptic curves. This paper gives a new proof using the Bertolini–Darmon–Prasanna formula, covering both ordinary and non-ordinary primes and higher-weight cases previously out of reach, broadening the settings where this deep arithmetic relationship is rigorously established.

math.NT
Original abstract

This paper provides a new proof of the $p$-adic Gross--Zagier formula for the $p$-adic $L$-function associated with the base change of a normalised cuspidal eigen-newform $f$ of weight $k \geq 2$ (and families of such) to an imaginary quadratic field $K$. Our results encompass both the classical $p$-ordinary cases and non-ordinary scenarios, including new cases where $k > 2$ and $\mathrm{ord}_p(a_p(f)) > 0$. Unlike the traditional approach of comparing geometric and analytic kernels, we employ a ``wall-crossing'' strategy centred on the BDP formula and the theory of Beilinson--Flach elements.

An inverse theorem for sumsets of sets of positive density in the integers

Ethan Ackelsberg, Florian K. Richter

If A and B are sets of positive integers, their sumset A+B has density at least d(A)+d(B) by Kneser's inequality—but when does equality hold? This paper completely characterizes such extremal pairs outside the 'obvious' arithmetic progression cases, answering a long-standing question of Erdős and Graham. The result is an inverse theorem: equality forces a very specific, rigid structure on A and B.

math.NT math.CO math.DS
Original abstract

Let $d(\cdot)$ denote the natural density on the positive integers. We characterize all sets $A,B$ with positive density satisfying $d(A+B)=d(A)+d(B)$, under the assumption that the two sets are not both contained in a proper finite union of residue classes. This gives a new inverse theorem for Kneser's sumset inequality in the integers, and provides a partial answer to a long-standing open question of Erdős and Graham.

Applications of patching the coherent cohomology of modular curves

Chengyang Bao

This paper applies a powerful algebraic technique—Taylor–Wiles–Kisin patching—to the geometry of modular curves, objects central to modern number theory. The key outputs are new cases where crystalline deformation rings (algebraic objects encoding p-adic Galois representations) satisfy a strong regularity condition called Cohen–Macaulay, plus a density result for special points. These advance the geometric side of the Langlands program connecting number theory and representation theory.

math.NT
Original abstract

In this paper, we apply the Taylor--Wiles--Kisin patching method to the coherent cohomology of modular curves at minimal level. We establish a multiplicity-one result for the patched module by the $q$-expansion principle and show that a certain partial normalization of the crystalline deformation ring is Cohen--Macaulay. As applications, we prove new cases where crystalline deformation rings are Cohen--Macaulay, establish a Zariski density result for crystalline points in characteristic $p$, and prove a multiplicity-one result for Serre's mod-$p$ quaternionic modular forms.

Simultaneous non-vanishing of Dirichlet L-functions

Hung M. Bui, Alexandra Florea, Micah B. Milinovich

Dirichlet L-functions are generalizations of the Riemann zeta function encoding number-theoretic data about primes in arithmetic progressions, and their values at the critical line s=1/2 are deeply mysterious. This paper proves, assuming the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis, that a positive proportion of characters χ make four L-functions simultaneously nonzero at any point on the critical line. Simultaneous nonvanishing results like this are rare and have implications for the distribution of primes.

math.NT
Original abstract

In this paper, we prove the simultaneous non-vanishing of four Dirichlet $L$-functions at any point on the critical line. More precisely, let $χ_1,\ldots,χ_4$ be even Dirichlet characters modulo $D_1,\ldots, D_4$ respectively, where the $D_j$ are pairwise co-prime and square-free integers. Under the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis, we prove that $\prod_{j=1}^4 L(1/2+it,χχ_j) \neq 0$ for a positive proportion of Dirichlet characters $χ\pmod q$, with $q$ prime and sufficiently large in terms of the $D_j$ and $t$ (and with an explicit relationship between $D_j, t$ and $q$). Unconditionally, we also prove a simultaneous non-vanishing result for four Dirichlet $L$-functions for infinitely many characters $χ\pmod q$, though in this case the proportion tends to zero as $q \to \infty$.

The level-8 Apery-limit and a proof of the Ramanujan Machine conjecture Z1

Alex Shvets

Does a mysterious ratio involving a combinatorial sum converge to a known constant? Yes: it converges to (7/32)ζ(3), where ζ(3) is Apéry's constant (the sum of reciprocal cubes). This settles a conjecture from the Ramanujan Machine project about a specific continued fraction equaling 8/(7ζ(3)). The proof blends modular forms, Wronskian identities, and Mellin-Barnes integrals, advancing our understanding of how continued fractions encode special values.

math.NT
Original abstract

We prove the level-8 Apery-limit lim B_n^{(8)}/s_n = (7/32) zeta(3), where s_n = sum_{k=0}^n C(n,k)^2 C(2k,n)^2 and B_n^{(8)} is the rational companion sequence. As a corollary we prove the Ramanujan Machine continued-fraction identity PCF((2n+1)(3n^2+3n+1), -n^6) = 8/(7 zeta(3)). The argument uses the level-8 eta-product parametrization, a Wronskian identity, Eichler integrals, and Mellin-Barnes extraction of the period polynomial. The value (7/32) zeta(3) was identified by Almkvist-van Straten-Zudilin (2008) and stated by Golyshev (arXiv:0908.1458); this paper gives the first complete proof. The companion level-6 result was proved in arXiv:2604.06239.

Branched covers of $\mathbb{P}^1$ and divisibility in class group

Kalyan Banerjee, Kalyan Chakraborty, Azizul Hoque

Can geometric properties of algebraic curves over ℂ produce arithmetic information about number fields? This paper shows that n-torsion points in the Jacobian (a group encoding the curve's topology) of an m-gonal curve yield n-torsion elements in the class group of an associated number field, a measure of how far its ring of integers deviates from unique factorization. This connects complex geometry to algebraic number theory in a concrete, constructive way.

math.NT math.AG
Original abstract

We start with $n$-torsions in the Jacobian of an $m$-gonal curve and produce $n$-torsions in the class group of certain number field $K$.

On the Chevalley-Bass number of a field

Jean Gillibert, Florence Gillibert, Gabriele Ranieri

The Chevalley-Bass number measures how efficiently certain algebraic equations over a field can be solved — think of it as quantifying a field's 'arithmetic complexity.' This paper establishes new upper and lower bounds on this number for characteristic-zero fields and provides an algorithm to compute it, with a concrete payoff: sharpening a key constant appearing in the study of exponential Diophantine equations.

math.NT
Original abstract

We give upper and lower bounds on the Chevalley-Bass number of a field of characteristic zero, whenever this quantity is well-defined. We also describe an algorithm which computes the Chevalley-Bass number of a field, provided its maximal abelian subextension is known. As a primary application, we improve the value of a constant related to exponential diophantine equations.

Rational analytic syntomic cohomology

Maximilian Hauck

Syntomic cohomology is a p-adic cohomology theory bridging de Rham and étale cohomology in p-adic geometry. This paper constructs its rational analytic version for rigid-analytic varieties (geometric spaces over p-adic fields), proving Poincaré duality, first Chern classes, and recovering classical p-adic Hodge theory comparison theorems from this unified framework.

math.AG math.NT
Original abstract

We define and study the rational analytic syntomification $X^{\mathrm{Syn}}$ of a partially proper rigid-analytic variety $X$ over $\mathbb{Q}_p$. We establish Poincaré duality and a theory of first Chern classes for the resulting cohomology theory, identify vector bundles on $X^{\mathrm{Syn}}$ with de Rham bundles on the Fargues--Fontaine curve of $X^{\diamondsuit}$ and recover several classical comparison theorems in $p$-adic Hodge theory. We also develop analogues of our results and constructions over $\mathbb{C}_p$.

The Very General Verra Fourfold is Irrational

Aideen Fay

A Verra fourfold is a specific 4-dimensional algebraic variety; 'irrational' means it cannot be mapped bijectively to ordinary 4-dimensional projective space. This paper proves the very general Verra fourfold is irrational using Hodge atoms — a framework encoding quantum cohomological data — marking the first application of this method to a variety with Picard rank greater than one (meaning it has a richer curve structure). This advances the classification of which higher-dimensional spaces are genuinely more complex than projective space.

math.AG
Original abstract

We show that the very general Verra fourfold is irrational, using the Hodge atom framework of Katzarkov--Kontsevich--Pantev--Yu. Two novel points are: a refined analysis of Hodge atoms, based on the involution on the cohomology of $X$, and a derivation of the quantum multiplication matrix from the quantum differential operator. This gives the first successful application of the method of Hodge atoms to a space with Picard rank greater than one.

Local vanishing for F-pure threefolds

Tatsuro Kawakami

Can classical vanishing theorems from complex algebraic geometry—tools ensuring certain cohomology groups are zero—be transplanted to characteristic p geometry, where standard methods break down? This paper establishes Grauert–Riemenschneider and Steenbrink vanishing for F-pure threefolds (a positive-characteristic analogue of log-canonical singularities) when p > 5. A key payoff is the logarithmic extension of differential one-forms, a foundational result for studying singularities in this setting.

math.AG
Original abstract

We establish Grauert--Riemenschneider vanishing for $F$-pure threefolds over a perfect field $k$ of characteristic $p>5$. We apply this to prove Steenbrink vanishing for three-dimensional sharply $F$-pure pairs in characteristic $p>5$. As a consequence, we obtain the logarithmic extension for one-forms in this setting.

Bernstein--Sato Theory for D-modules in Positive Characteristic

Daichi Takeuchi

Bernstein-Sato theory classically attaches polynomial invariants to singularities over the complex numbers; this paper builds an analogous theory for D-modules (systems encoding differential equations) over fields of prime characteristic p, defining their invariants as p-adic integers. The authors show this framework unifies and extends previous partial results. This matters because it opens a systematic bridge between characteristic-zero singularity theory and the tools of commutative algebra in positive characteristic.

math.AG
Original abstract

In this article, we develop a positive characteristic analogue of the Bernstein--Sato theory for holonomic D-modules in the complex setting. We work with D-modules on a Noetherian regular $F$-finite $\mathbb{F}_p$-scheme $X$, and define their Bernstein--Sato roots as $p$-adic integers. When the D-module is the structure sheaf $O_X$, this recovers Bitoun's definition. When the D-module arises from a locally finitely generated unit $F^e$-module and $X$ is of finite type over an $F$-finite field, we show that the roots are finite and rational, generalizing Bitoun's result. In the course of the proof, we also develop a related theory for Cartier modules.

Relative Langlands duality and Koszul duality

Alexander Braverman, Michael Finkelberg, Roman Travkin

The Langlands program connects symmetry groups to number theory; its 'relative' version involves symmetry groups acting on geometric spaces called hyperspherical varieties. Assuming a conjectural duality between S-dual pairs of such spaces, the authors derive an equivalence between certain representation-theoretic categories via Koszul duality—a powerful algebraic tool relating a mathematical structure to its 'dual' algebraic opposite—advancing the geometric foundations of the relative Langlands program.

math.AG math.RT math.SG
Original abstract

Consider a pair of $S$-dual hyperspherical varieties $G\circlearrowright X$ and $G^\vee\circlearrowright X^\vee$ equipped with equivariant quantizations $Q(X)$, $Q(X^\vee)$. Assume that the local conjecture of Ben-Zvi, Sakellaridis and Venkatesh holds for this pair, and also that $X\simeq T^*_ψ(Y)$ is polarized, so that $Q(X)=D_ψ(Y)$. Let $B\subset G$ (resp. $B^\vee\subset G^\vee$) be Borel subgroups. Then we deduce an equivalence between the ${\mathbb Z}/2$-graded $B$-equivariant category $(D_ψ(Y)\operatorname{-mod})^{{\mathbb Z}/2})^B$ and the ${\mathbb Z}/2$-graded unipotent $B^\vee$-monodromic category $(Q(X^\vee)\operatorname{-mod}^{{\mathbb Z}/2})^{B^\vee,\operatorname{mon}}$.

Mumford vanishing for threefolds in positive characteristic

Tatsuro Kawakami, Hiromu Tanaka

Vanishing theorems tell us when certain cohomology groups—algebraic invariants measuring 'holes' in a space—are trivially zero, simplifying geometric analysis. In characteristic zero (classical algebraic geometry), Kawamata-Viehweg vanishing is a cornerstone tool, but it fails in positive characteristic. This paper proves Mumford vanishing for klt threefolds (mildly singular 3D varieties) in characteristic p>5, recovering key cases of this vanishing in settings where standard tools break down.

math.AG
Original abstract

Let $X$ be a projective klt threefold in characteristic $p>5$ and let $L$ be a nef Cartier divisor on $X$. We show that $H^1(X, -L)=0$ for the following two cases: (1) $K_X$ is not big and $L$ is big; (2) $-K_X$ is nef and $L$ is of numerical dimension two.

On the large genus of Hurwitz numbers

Davide Accadia, Danilo Lewański, Giulio Ruzza

Hurwitz numbers count branched covers of surfaces—essentially ways a surface can 'wrap around' another with specified folding behavior. This paper develops an elementary, unified framework for computing how these counts behave as the genus (topological complexity) grows large, applying it to many variants simultaneously. The result matters because large-genus asymptotics connect to random matrix theory and topological recursion, and previous approaches required heavy machinery for each individual case.

math.AG math.RT
Original abstract

Hurwitz theory provides a large variety of enumerative problems related to algebraic geometry, mathematical physics, and combinatorics. We give a general framework to approach the large genus asymptotics of Hurwitz theory using only elementary methods and apply it to several types of Hurwitz numbers: single, double, or with an arbitrary numbers of fixed ramifications; simple and / or including completed cycles type of ramification and / or finitely many blocks of weakly monotone and / or strictly monotone types of ramifications. These, to the best of our knowledge, cover most of the Hurwitz numbers studied, and include for instance correlators of the HCIZ matrix model, Grothendieck dessins d'enfant, weighted Hurwitz numbers, and Gromov-Witten invariants of the Riemann sphere. We also apply our method to b-content Hurwitz numbers. As a specialisation, we recover some previously known about the large genus asymptotics of Hurwitz theory, namely classical results by Hurwitz and recent results of Do-He-Robertson, C. Yang, and results connected to recent work of X. Li.

Symmetric subrank and its border analogue

Benjamin Biaggi, Jan Draisma, Koen de Nooij et al.

The symmetric subrank of a polynomial measures how close it is to a sum of pure powers—a natural complexity measure in algebraic geometry and tensor theory. This paper determines exactly how symmetric subrank behaves asymptotically as the number of variables grows, using geometric invariant theory. The result extends analogous theorems for ordinary tensors, revealing universal growth rates governing polynomial complexity.

math.AG cs.CC math.AC
Original abstract

The symmetric subrank of homogeneous polynomial is the largest number of terms in a diagonal form to which it can be specialized by a (typically non-invertible) linear variable substitution. Building on earlier work by Derksen-Makam-Zuiddam and Biaggi-Chang-Draisma-Rupniewski for ordinary tensors, we determine the asymptotic behavior of symmetric subrank and symmetric border subrank of degree-d forms as the number of variables tends to infinity. Furthermore, by using results from geometric invariant theory we show that for cubic (resp. quartic) forms the symmetric subrank and symmetric border subrank coincide if the latter is at most three (resp. two).

Categorical Torelli theorems for higher Picard rank Fano double covers

Augustinas Jacovskis, Reinder Meinsma

A categorical Torelli theorem asks whether a Fano threefold (a special complex algebraic variety) can be reconstructed from an abstract categorical piece of its derived category called the Kuznetsov component. This paper proves such theorems for three families of Fano threefolds that are double covers of simpler spaces, branched over K3 surfaces. The key move is reducing equivalences of Kuznetsov components to isomorphisms of the branch K3 surfaces, then lifting back to the threefolds.

math.AG
Original abstract

We prove categorical Torelli theorems for three families of Fano threefolds with Picard rank $ρ>1$. Members of these families manifest as double covers branched in anticanonical K3 surfaces. Our proof is based on reducing equivalences between the Kuznetsov components of Fano threefolds in the same deformation family to derive equivalences of their respective K3 branch divisors, and deducing that the resulting isomorphism of branch divisors gives rise to an isomorphism of the Fano threefolds for each family.

Semiorthogonal decompositions and components of derived categories of orthogonal Grassmannian fibrations

Saket Shah

Semiorthogonal decompositions break a variety's derived category—a homological invariant encoding its geometry—into simpler pieces. For fibrations whose fibers are orthogonal Grassmannians (spaces of isotropic subspaces for a quadratic form), this paper embeds the derived category of an associated Clifford algebra as a canonical component, generalizing Kuznetsov's theorem for quadric fibrations. This advances a broad program to understand derived categories of structured geometric families.

math.AG math.RT
Original abstract

Kuznetsov showed that for a flat quadric fibration $\mathcal{Q}$ over a smooth base $S$, $\mathrm{D}^b(\mathcal{Q})$ admits a semiorthogonal decomposition where one of the components is the derived category of the sheaf of even parts of a Clifford algebra $\mathrm{D}^b(S,\mathcal{C}l_0)$. \par As progress towards a generalization, we show that for a quadric fibration with a fairly minor condition on the rank of the quadric fibers, the category $\mathrm{D}^b(S,\mathcal{C}l_0)$ embeds fully faithfully into the derived category of the relative orthogonal Grassmannian $\mathrm{D}^b(\mathrm{OGr}(k,\mathcal{Q}))$. When $k = 2$, we use this to produce a semiorthogonal decomposition of $\mathrm{D}^b(\mathrm{OGr}(2,\mathcal{Q}))$ up to a residual category; we compute this residual category in the smooth case and produce a conjecture for in the case of a pencil of quadrics with smooth base locus.

Affine Springer fiber and the small quantum group

Roman Bezrukavnikov, Pablo Boixeda Alvarez, Michael McBreen et al.

The principal block of a quantum group at a root of unity—a key algebraic object in representation theory—gets a new geometric home: microsheaves on an affine Springer fiber, a geometric space encoding Lie-theoretic data. This lets the authors prove a wild-ramification geometric Langlands equivalence and connect it to mirror symmetry. The result bridges quantum groups, geometric Langlands, and homological mirror symmetry in one framework.

math.AG math.RT
Original abstract

We find a new geometric incarnation for the principal block in the category of modules over a quantum group at a root of unity, realizing it as a full subcategory of microsheaves on a certain affine Springer fiber. We also prove a related geometric Langlands type equivalence with wild ramification, identifying the latter category with a category of coherent sheaves on the Springer resolution for the dual group. This can also be viewed as a version of homological mirror symmetry for the Springer resolution.

Asymptotic Behavior of Tropical Rank Functions

Ana Maria Botero, Alex Küronya, Eduardo Vital

Tropical geometry replaces classical curves with combinatorial graphs, and 'rank' of a divisor measures how many independent functions it controls. This paper shows that two competing tropical rank notions share the same large-scale asymptotics, governed by a single invariant called tropical volume—mirroring how volumes govern line bundles in classical algebraic geometry. The tropical volume is also shown to be compatible with the tropicalization map from classical curves.

math.AG math.CO

We show that the asymptotic behavior of the two main competing notions of rank of a linear series on a tropical curve is governed by a single invariant, closely paralleling the theory of volumes in algebraic geometry. In addition, we show that the tropical volume is compatible with the tropicalization of curves.

Elena Kopylova

The Klein–Gordon equation describes relativistic quantum particles, and dispersive estimates quantify how solutions spread out over time in Lᵖ norms. This paper sharpens the known decay rates for the one-dimensional case—achieving stronger norm estimates under weaker assumptions on the potential—via a new analytical approach that could inform broader studies of nonlinear wave equations.

Landau damping on expanding backgrounds

David Fajman, Liam Urban

Does Landau damping — the phenomenon where plasma oscillations decay without dissipation — survive in an expanding universe? This paper proves it does for the Vlasov-Poisson system (modeling self-gravitating charged particles) when the universe expands as a power law t^q with q between 0 and 1/2, provided initial data is small in a Gevrey class (a strong smoothness condition). This connects plasma physics to cosmological models where expansion competes with nonlinear instabilities.

math.AP gr-qc math-ph
Original abstract

We analyse the effect of expansion in Newtonian cosmology on the asymptotic behaviour of charged self-interacting plasmas close to Poisson equilibria. To this end, we study the Vlasov-Poisson system on the phase space of a $3$-torus which is expanding with respect to the scale factor $a(t)$. We show that, for $a(t)=t^q$ with $q\in(0,\frac12)$, solutions to this system exhibit nonlinear Landau damping for initial data that is small with respect to a suitably strong Gevrey class, i.e., the charge density contrast of the plasma decays superpolynomially. For larger choices of $q$ within this range, the initial data requirements become stricter while the decay weakens. To our knowledge, this is the first result showing Landau damping in a cosmological setting.

The energy-critical stochastic nonlinear Schrödinger equation: well-posedness and blow-up

Annie Millet, Svetlana Roudenko

Can the nonlinear Schrödinger equation, a model for wave propagation, be solved when random noise is added at the energy-critical threshold — the hardest analytic regime? This paper establishes local well-posedness (existence and uniqueness of solutions) for both focusing and defocusing cases under additive or multiplicative noise, and derives blow-up criteria showing when solutions can collapse in finite time. The energy-critical setting is particularly delicate because standard perturbative tools break down.

math.AP math.PR
Original abstract

We investigate the focusing and defocusing energy-critical stochastic nonlinear Schrödinger equation, subject to random perturbations in the form of either additive or multiplicative (Stratonovich) noise. We establish local well-posedness for random or deterministic initial data $u_0$ in $\dot{H}^1(\mathbb{R}^n)$ or $H^1(\mathbb{R}^n)$, depending on the noise type. In the focusing case we provide quantitative estimates regarding the existence time and probability. Moreover, we derive blow-up criteria for solutions with positive energy in both cases of noise, provided that the noise intensity is sufficiently small, showing that blow-up occurs before a certain given positive time with positive probability, thus, extending deterministic results of Kenig-Merle [24] for the energy-critical NLS equation to the stochastic setting.

Homogenization of the Navier-Stokes equations in a randomly perforated domain in the inviscid limit

Richard M. Höfer, Eleni Hübner-Rosenau

What happens to fluid flow governed by Navier-Stokes equations when viscosity vanishes and the fluid fills a domain riddled with randomly placed holes? This paper proves quantitative convergence results describing the limiting behavior, with the outcome depending sensitively on hole size versus viscosity scaling. Simultaneously handling both the homogenization limit (averaging over microstructure) and the inviscid limit is technically novel and resolves a previously open regime of this double-limit problem.

math.AP
Original abstract

We study the behaviour of the solution $u_\varepsilon$ to the Navier-Stokes equations with vanishing viscosity and a non-slip condition in a randomly perforated domain. We consider the space $\mathbb{R}^3$ where we remove $N$ holes that are i.i.d. distributed. The behaviour depends on the particle size $\varepsilon^α=N^{-α/3}$ and the viscosity $\varepsilon^γ=N^{-γ/3}$ of the fluid. We prove quantitative convergence results to a function $u$, provided that the local Reynolds number is small, in the subcritical ($α+γ>3$) and critical ($α+γ=3$) regime. The nonlinear Schrödinger equation with a white noise potential—a randomly rough, spatially irregular coefficient—is notoriously hard to solve rigorously in dimensions d≤3. Using paracontrolled calculus (a framework for handling singular stochastic terms) and dispersive Strichartz estimates, this paper establishes existence and uniqueness of energy solutions, resolving well-posedness for these physically relevant but mathematically rough equations.

Original abstract

In this paper, we prove existence and uniqueness of energy solutions for nonlinear Schrödinger equations with a multiplicative white noise on $R^d$ with $d\le3$. We rely on an exponential trans-form and conserved quantities for existence of energy solutions. Using paracontrolled calculus, we prove Strichartz inequalities which encode the dispersive properties of the solutions. This allows to obtain local well-posedness for low regularity solutions and uniqueness of energy solutions for various equations. In particular, our results are the first results of propagation without loss of both regularity and localization for such equations in full space as well as the first results on $R^3$ for such singular dispersive SPDEs. Original abstract

We consider the interacting Bose gas in the thermodynamic limit in a large box in $\R^d$ at positive temperature $1/β\in(0,\infty)$ with particle density $\simρ\in(0,\infty)$.
