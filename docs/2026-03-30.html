Iterated beta integrals

Minoru Hirose, Nobuo Sato

Iterated beta integrals are a newly defined class of integrals that generalize two classical objects — hyperlogarithms (iterated logarithmic integrals appearing in particle physics) and beta integrals — into a single unified framework. The key property is invariance under shifting all exponent parameters simultaneously, which generates surprising relations between these integrals. This unification opens new paths for studying special functions and their number-theoretic properties.

math.NT
Original abstract

We introduce iterated beta integrals, a new class of iterated integrals on the universal abelian covering of the punctured projective line that unifies hyperlogarithms and classical beta integrals while preserving their fundamental properties. We establish various analytic properties of these integrals with respect to both the exponent parameters and the main variables. Their key feature is invariance under simultaneous translation of the exponent parameters, which generates relations between integrals over possibly different coverings. This mechanism recovers notable identities for multiple zeta values and variants -- including Zagier's 2-3-2 formula, Murakami's $t$-value analogue, Charlton's $t$-value analogue, Zhao's $2$-$1$ formula, and Ohno's relation -- and also yields new relations, such as a proof of a Galois descent phenomenon for multiple omega values.

An integrality phenomenon

Florian Fürnsinn, Danylo Radchenko, Wadim Zudilin

The paper proves a general integrality result: sequences satisfying certain linear recursions with polynomial coefficients always consist of integers, settling a broad class of conjectures at once. Integrality of such sequences is subtle because the recursion involves division by n, so cancellation must occur for non-obvious reasons. As a highlight, the result confirms a conjectured integrality for a sequence tied to the Hörmander–Bernhardsson extremal function, an object from complex analysis.

math.NT math.CA math.CO
Original abstract

We prove a general statement about the integrality of the sequences generated by a recursion of the following form: $nu_n$ equals a linear combination of $u_{n-1},u_{n-2},\dots,u_0$ with polynomial coefficients in $n$ of special form. This includes a conjectural integrality of the sequence related to the Hörmander-Bernhardsson extremal function, for which we further give a direct proof as well.

Igusa Stacks and the Cohomology of Shimura Varieties II

Patrick Daniels, Pol van Hoften, Dongryul Kim et al.

Shimura varieties are arithmetic objects connecting number theory and geometry, and understanding their cohomology is central to the Langlands program—a vast web of conjectures linking number theory and representation theory. This paper constructs Igusa stacks (geometric tools organizing p-adic symmetries) for all abelian-type Shimura varieties, then uses them to prove the Fargues–Scholze local Langlands correspondence matches classical constructions for all classical groups of types A, B, and D.

math.NT math.AG math.RT
Original abstract

We construct Igusa stacks for all Shimura varieties of abelian type and derive consequences for the cohomology of these Shimura varieties. As an application, we prove that the Fargues--Scholze local Langlands correspondence agrees with the semi-simplification of the local Langlands correspondences constructed by Arthur, Mok and others, for all classical groups of type $A$, $B$ and $D$; this extends work of Hamann, Bertoloni Meli--Hamann--Nguyen and Peng.

The second minimum of Barnes-Wall lattices

Gabriele Nebe

Barnes-Wall lattices are a family of highly symmetric sphere-packing lattices in dimensions that are powers of 2. The 'minimum' is the shortest nonzero vector length, and the 'second minimum' is the next distinct length. This paper proves the second minimum is at least 3/2 times the minimum, establishing a quantitative gap between the two shortest scales and contributing to understanding the fine structure of these important lattices.

math.NT
Original abstract

We show that the second minimum of the Barnes-Wall lattices is a least $3/2$ of the minimum.

On $\frac{1}{n!}$ in Cantor sets

Kehao Lin, Yufeng Wu, Siyu Yang

The middle-third Cantor set is built by repeatedly removing middle thirds of intervals, leaving a fractal set of real numbers with a clean base-3 description. This paper proves that only 1 and 1/120 among all factorial reciprocals 1/n! land in this set, answering an open question. The method generalizes: any 'missing-digit set' (built by forbidding certain digits in some base) contains only finitely many factorial reciprocals, all explicitly findable.

math.NT
Original abstract

Let $C$ be the middle-third Cantor set. We show that \[\left\{\frac{1}{n!}: n\in\mathbb{N}\right\}\cap C=\left\{1, \frac{1}{5!}\right\}.\] This answers a question recently posed by Jiang [J. Lond. Math. Soc., 2026, published online]. Our approach generalizes to general missing-digit sets, showing that, in any such set, there are only finitely many elements of the form $\frac{1}{n!}$, all of which can be effectively determined.

Anticyclotomic Iwasawa main conjectures for modular forms

Matteo Longo, Maria Rosaria Pati, Stefano Vigni

Iwasawa main conjectures relate two different ways of measuring arithmetic complexity—L-functions and Selmer groups—over infinite towers of number fields. This paper proves these conjectures for modular forms over anticyclotomic extensions of imaginary quadratic fields (extensions encoding a specific symmetry), in both definite and indefinite settings, under mild conditions. This significantly advances our understanding of the arithmetic of modular forms via p-adic methods.

math.NT math.AG
Original abstract

Let $f$ be a newform of even weight at least $4$, level $N$ and trivial character. Let $p\nmid N$ be an odd prime number that is ordinary for $f$ and let $K$ be an imaginary quadratic field satisfying a generalized Heegner hypothesis relative to $N$. In this paper, we prove (under mild arithmetic assumptions) Iwasawa main conjectures for $f$ over the anticyclotomic $\mathbb Z_p$-extension of $K$ both in the definite setting and in the indefinite setting (in the second case, we prove a main conjecture à la Perrin-Riou for modular forms). Our strategy of proof follows the approach of Bertolini-Darmon via congruences combined with our previous results on an analogue for $f$ of Kolyvagin's conjecture on the non-triviality of his $p$-adic system of derived Heegner points on elliptic curves. As a second contribution, when $p$ splits in $K$ we prove an Iwasawa-Greenberg main conjecture for the $p$-adic $L$-functions of Bertolini-Darmon-Prasanna and Brooks.

The de Rham and the syntomic logarithm

Matthias Flach, Achim Krause, Baptiste Morin

The Bloch-Kato exponential map is a fundamental tool in p-adic Hodge theory connecting algebraic K-theory to cohomology; this paper constructs a precise integral inverse called the de Rham logarithm. Using filtered prismatic cohomology, the authors prove a broad version of the Beilinson fibre square — a key structural result relating different cohomology theories — for a very general class of geometric objects. This extends important foundational results to a significantly wider setting.

math.NT
Original abstract

We define and study an integral refinement of the inverse of the Bloch-Kato exponential map which we call the de Rham logarithm. Our main tool to analyze the de Rham logarithm is the syntomic logarithm, a certain limit construction based on the theory of filtered prismatic cohomology initiated by Antieau, Krause and Nikolaus. We use the syntomic logarithm to prove a version of the Beilinson fibre square for all quasicompact, quasiseparated derived formal schemes. We also use our techniques to prove Conjecture $C_{EP}(\bq_p(n))$ of Fontaine and Perrin-Riou for all local fields $K/\bq_p$ and to compute the correction factor $C(X,n)$ introduced by Flach and Morin in their reformulation of the Bloch-Kato Tamagawa number conjecture for the Zeta function of a smooth projective scheme $X$ over a number ring.

A categorical Torelli theorem for quartic del Pezzo surfaces

Alexey Elagin

Can you reconstruct a geometric space from an algebraic shadow? This paper proves that quartic del Pezzo surfaces — degree-4 algebraic surfaces — are uniquely determined by their Kuznetsov component, a triangulated subcategory of the derived category encoding 'leftover' algebraic data after stripping away basic sheaves. The result works over any perfect field and in equivariant settings, advancing the categorical Torelli program.

math.AG
Original abstract

We solve categorical Torelli problem for quartic del Pezzo surfaces. That is, we prove that a del Pezzo surface of degree $4$ can be canonically reconstructed from its Kuznetsov component, which is the orthogonal subcategory to the structure sheaf in the derived category of the surface. Our methods work in equivariant setting and over arbitrary perfect fields. Using recent theory of atomic semi-orthogonal decompositions arXiv:2512.05064, we conclude that two minimal quartic del Pezzo surfaces are birational if and only if they are isomorphic. We also verify that the Kuznetsov component of a minimal quartic del Pezzo surface is semi-orthogonally indecomposable, confirming a conjecture by Auel and Bernardara.

A birational version of K-stability for big classes

Ruadhaí Dervan, Rémi Reboulet

K-stability is a algebraic-geometric condition that predicts when a variety admits a canonical metric, but the classical theory requires an ample line bundle (one with strong positivity). This paper extends uniform K-stability to 'big' line bundles — a strictly larger class — and proves it can be detected by finitely many divisorial valuations (algebraic measurements of singularities). The result broadens the landscape of the Yau–Tian–Donaldson conjecture to a significantly more general setting.

math.AG
Original abstract

We introduce a theory of uniform K-stability for big line bundles on smooth projective varieties. This extends the existing theory both for varieties with ample line bundles, and for varieties with big anticanonical class. Our main result gives a valuative characterisation of uniform K-stability, through finite collections of divisorial valuations. We further prove that uniform K-stability is preserved under pullbacks and certain pushforwards, which implies that uniform K-stability is well-defined at the level of b-divisors.

Weighted Cohomology, Hodge Theory and Intersection Cohomology of Shimura varieties

Mingyu Ni

Shimura varieties are arithmetically rich geometric spaces whose cohomology encodes deep number-theoretic information, but their non-compactness complicates analysis. This paper identifies the intersection cohomology of a standard compactification with a specific piece of the mixed Hodge structure on another compactification, establishing natural algebraic operations and linking geometric volumes of these spaces to topological invariants via special cycle classes.

math.AG math.NT
Original abstract

We prove that the intersection cohomology of the Baily-Borel compactification of a complex Shimura variety is identified with the top weight quotient of the mixed Hodge structure on the reductive Borel-Serre compactification. This yields canonical cup products and functorial pullbacks on the intersection cohomology. As an application, we introduce canonical cycle classes associated to special cycles, relating analytic geometric volumes of non-compact Shimura varieties to topological terms.

Stein degree of proper morphisms

Caucher Birkar

How do you measure the 'size' of a map between geometric spaces when it isn't a simple finite covering? This paper develops the notion of Stein degree for proper morphisms (maps between algebraic varieties that behave nicely at infinity) by isolating the finite part via Stein factorization. The invariant connects naturally to singularity theory and the minimal model program, central tools in classifying higher-dimensional algebraic varieties.

math.AG
Original abstract

The notion of degree begins in field theory as the dimension of a field extension. In algebraic geometry, this idea reappears as the degree of a finite morphism, defined using the induced extension of function fields. For proper morphisms that are not necessarily finite, Stein factorization isolates the finite part of the map and leads to the notion of Stein degree. This invariant is especially useful in birational geometry, where it interacts naturally with singularities of pairs and the study of log Calabi-Yau fibrations. In this article we give an expository introduction to these ideas, discuss motivating examples, and explain a boundedness problem for Stein degree arising in recent work of the author and collaborators.

Non-abelian Hodge theory for non-proper varieties and the linear Shafarevich conjecture

Benjamin Bakker

Non-abelian Hodge theory connects representations of fundamental groups of algebraic varieties to geometric objects called Higgs bundles. This survey explains recent progress extending these tools to non-compact varieties, then uses them to tackle the linear Shafarevich conjecture—which predicts that any algebraic variety with a linear fundamental group admits a map to a lower-dimensional variety capturing its essential topology—proving a general version for all algebraic varieties.

math.AG
Original abstract

We survey recent advances in non-abelian Hodge theory in the "mixed" setting of non-proper algebraic varieties. We then describe how these tools are used to construct algebraic Shafarevich morphisms and prove a version of the linear Shafarevich conjecture for any algebraic variety.

The Weil Decoration of the Horrocks-Mumford Bundle

Klaus Altmann, Andreas Hochenegger, Frederik Witt

The Horrocks-Mumford bundle is a famous, exotic rank-2 vector bundle on 4-dimensional projective space with no known analog in higher dimensions. This paper introduces 'Weil decorations' — a way to encode higher-rank reflexive sheaves (generalized vector bundles) using divisor-like data — and uses this framework to construct a natural generalization of the Horrocks-Mumford bundle to broader settings.

math.AG
Original abstract

For a normal algebraic variety we generalise the relation between reflexive rank one sheaves and Weil divisors to reflexive sheaves of arbitrary rank and so-called Weil decorations. As an application, we define and study a natural generalisation of the celebrated Horrocks-Mumford bundle.

Computing the Skyscraper Invariant

Marc Fersztand, Jan Jendrysiak

Multiparameter persistence modules are algebraic summaries of data shape across multiple scales, and the Skyscraper Invariant refines their comparison using algebraic filtrations. This paper introduces the first practical algorithms to compute it, exploiting structure specific to persistence modules to speed up computation over naive approaches. This advances the toolkit for topological data analysis, where computing fine-grained shape invariants efficiently remains a core challenge.

cs.DS math.AG math.AT math.RT
Original abstract

We develop the first algorithms for computing the Skyscraper Invariant [FJNT24]. This is a filtration of the classical rank invariant for multiparameter persistence modules defined by the Harder-Narasimhan filtrations along every central charge supported at a single parameter value. Cheng's algorithm [Cheng24] can be used to compute HN filtrations of arbitrary acyclic quiver representations in polynomial time in the total dimension, but in practice, the large dimension of persistence modules makes this direct approach infeasible. We show that by exploiting the additivity of the HN filtration and the special central charges, one can get away with a brute-force approach. For $d$-parameter modules, this produces an FPT $\varepsilon$-approximate algorithm with runtime dominated by $O( 1/\varepsilon^d \cdot T_{\mathsc{dec}})$, where $T_{\mathsc{dec}}$ is the time for decomposition, which we compute with \textsc{aida} [DJK25]. We show that the wall-and-chamber structure of the module can be computed via lower envelopes of degree $d - 1$ polynomials. This allows for an exact computation of the Skyscraper Invariant whose runtime is roughly $O(n^d \cdot T_{\mathsc{dec}})$ for $n$ the size of the presentation of the modules and enables a faster hybrid algorithm to compute an approximation. For 2-parameter modules, we have implemented not only our algorithms but also, for the first time, Cheng's algorithm. We compare all algorithms and, as a proof of concept for data analysis, compute a filtered version of the Multiparameter Landscape for biomedical data.

Shifting local exponents of Picard-Fuchs operators

Tymoteusz Chmiel

Picard-Fuchs operators are differential equations encoding how integrals vary in a family of geometric shapes called Calabi-Yau threefolds. The paper asks: when you 'shift' the local exponents (solutions' growth rates near singular points) of such an operator, do you still get an operator coming from genuine geometry? The authors characterize exactly which shifts preserve geometric origin, then use this to build operators with exotic properties—advancing our toolkit for studying mirror symmetry.

math.AG
Original abstract

We investigate the operation of shifting local exponents and study its effects on the monodromy representation of a one-parameter family of Calabi-Yau threefolds. The main result is a characterization of shifts of geometric operators which are also geometric. We use this description to construct some Picard-Fuchs operators with interesting properties.

The relative movable cone conjecture for K-trivial fibrations in varieties with well-clipped movable cones

Aurélien Faucher

The Kawamata-Morrison movable cone conjecture predicts that the symmetries of a certain geometric cone associated to a Calabi-Yau variety act 'almost periodically.' This paper proves a relative version for fibered spaces whose fibers are quotients of products of abelian varieties and certain surfaces. The result expands the territory where this influential conjecture is verified, using the new technical notion of 'well-clipped movable cones.'

math.AG
Original abstract

We prove the weak relative Kawamata-Morrison movable cone conjecture for K-trivial fibrations whose very general fibre is a quotient, by a finite group of automorphisms acting freely in codimension one, of a product of certain Calabi-Yau pairs whose underlying varieties have well-clipped movable cones, a notion recently introduced by Cécile Gachet. Our main result applies in particular when the fibre is a finite product of an abelian variety, smooth rational surfaces underlying klt Calabi-Yau pairs, projective irreducible holomorphic symplectic manifolds and Enriques manifolds, both of a known type. As a consequence, there are only finitely many minimal models over the base, up to isomorphism. When the relative movable cone is non-degenerate, we obtain the full relative movable cone conjecture.

Weil restriction, normal bundles and motivic Thom spaces

Xi Chen, Guangzhao Zhu

Norm functors in motivic homotopy theory — tools connecting algebraic geometry and homotopy theory — usually require heavy categorical machinery. This paper grounds them geometrically using Weil restriction, a classical operation turning varieties over a field extension into varieties over the base field. The payoff is elementary proofs that Weil restriction preserves vector bundles and extends results about normal bundles, making key motivic constructions more accessible.

math.AG math.AT
Original abstract

Recent developments in motivic homotopy theory, especially the construction of norm functors by Bachmann and Hoyois, rely heavily on the machinery of infinite categories. In this paper, we take a purely geometric and elementary approach via the Weil restriction of schemes -- the fundamental geometric operation underlying these norm functors -- without invoking highly abstract categorical methods. We show that the Weil restriction preserves vector bundles and extend an existing result on normal bundles. We then construct the Weil restriction functor on the unstable motivic homotopy category and prove its compatibility with Thom spaces. Finally, in the setting of effective motives and the associated cohomology theories, we show that the Weil restriction sends Thom classes to Thom classes.

The exceptional locus of a motivic local system

Luca Terenzi

How much does the 'motivic complexity' — a measure of algebraic-geometric structure — of a local system's fibers vary across a variety? This paper defines an exceptional locus capturing where complexity drops, and proves a motivic analogue of the Cattani–Deligne–Kaplan theorem: that locus is a countable union of algebraic subvarieties. This extends a classical result about Hodge theory into the finer world of motivic Galois groups.

math.AG math.NT
Original abstract

Given a Nori motivic local system over a smooth, connected complex algebraic variety, we define its exceptional locus as a way to measure the variation in the motivic complexity of its stalks. The definition is given explicitly in terms of motivic Galois groups and Artin motives. Our main result is a motivic analogue of the Cattani--Deligne--Kaplan Theorem, asserting that the exceptional locus is a countable union of closed algebraic subvarieties. Moreover, we show that the maximal such subvarieties are defined over any algebraically closed subfield over which the ambient variety and the motivic local system admit models, and that they are stable under Galois conjugation when these models descend to a further subfield. This strengthens and extends previous results by André in the pure case. We obtain a similar geometric description for the splitting locus of the motivic weight filtration. In the case of 1-motivic local systems, the above properties pass to the underlying variations of mixed Hodge structure via André's fullness theorem.

Constructions for rational multiple planes

Ciro Ciliberto, Rick Miranda

A 'multiple plane' is a branched cover of the projective plane, where the branch curve records where sheets collide. This paper constructs and classifies 'simple' such covers of degree ≥ 3 — ones where a nice family of rational curves pulls back to another rational family — analyzing the algebraic constraints the branch curve must satisfy. The work extends classical surface theory and clarifies which branching data can actually arise.

math.AG
Original abstract

A finite, normal cover $f: X\longrightarrow \bbP^2$ of degree $m\geq 3$ (the case $m=2$ is well known and we do not consider it in this paper) is called \emph{simple}, if there is a pencil $\mathcal P$ of rational curves of $\bbP^2$ such that the pull back via $f$ of $\mathcal P$ is a pencil of rational curves on $X$. Up to Cremona equivalence $\mathcal P$ can be assumed to be the pencil of lines through a fixed point $p\in \bbP^2$. If $\frakB$ is the branch curve of such a multiple plane, the general line through $p$ has to intersect $\frakB$ in $2m-2$ branch points (counted with multiplicities). If $p$ is not one of these branch points, then the multiple plane is said to be \ \emph{simpler}. \ In that case the branch curve will have a point of multiplicity $°(\frakB)-2m+2$ at $p$. In this paper we classify, under suitable generality conditions for the branch curve, { simpler } triple planes up to Cremona equivalence (they belong to infinitely many non--Cremona equivalent families) and we give examples of infinitely many non--Cremona equivalent families of {simpler } multiple planes of degree $m\geq 4$.

Neutral representations of finite diagonalizable group schemes and fields of moduli

Giulio Bresciani, Angelo Vistoli, Tianzhi Yang

When can you tell whether a geometric object has a rational point by examining its vector bundles? This paper introduces 'neutral representations'—special representations of a group G where the existence of a particular vector bundle forces a G-torsor to be trivial—and uses them to resolve fields-of-moduli versus fields-of-definition problems for algebraic curves. This addresses a classical question about when a geometric object can be defined over the smallest field it appears to live over.

math.AG math.NT
Original abstract

We introduce the notion of a neutral representation of a finite group, or finite group scheme, $G$; a representation $V$ with the property that if a gerbe $\mathcal{G}$ over a field $k$ that is a form of the classifying stack $\mathcal{B} G$ admits a vector bundle that is a form of $V$, then it is neutral, that is, $\mathcal{G}(k)$ is not empty. We give some criteria for a representation of a finite diagonalizable group scheme to be neutral. We apply this notion to give wide classes of examples of smooth curves, or varieties with a marked point, with cyclic automorphism groups, which are defined over their field of moduli, greatly generalizing some previous results.

The fundamental solution of a nonlinear kinetic Fokker-Planck equation

Giovanni Brigati, Guillaume Carlier, Jean Dolbeault

How does a nonlinear kinetic Fokker-Planck equation — which models particle diffusion in phase space with a porous-medium-type nonlinearity — evolve from a point-mass initial condition? The paper shows this fundamental solution behaves self-similarly, meaning it rescales into a fixed shape over time, and governs the long-time behavior of broad solution classes. This provides a complete analytic picture analogous to the Gaussian for the classical heat equation.

math.AP
Original abstract

This paper is devoted to a fundamental solution of a nonlinear kinetic equation involving a porous medium or fast diffusion operator acting on velocities. Such a nonlinearity has interesting scaling properties, which result in a self-similar behaviour of the fundamental solution. Here fundamental solution means a Dirac distribution initial datum which moreover governs the large time asymptotics of a large class of solutions. Using a self-similar change of variables, the equation becomes a nonlinear kinetic Fokker-Planck equation with harmonic confinement and the intermediate asymptotics regime is transformed into a stability property of a special stationary solution, which attracts the solutions for large times. In the homogeneous case (pure nonlinear diffusion), the problem is reduced to a classical nonlinear diffusion equation with Barenblatt-Pattle self-similar profiles. Unexpectedly, this beautiful structure is preserved at kinetic level, with remarkable consequences for relative entropy estimates, detailed intermediate asymptotics and nonlinear diffusion limits in adapted functional spaces.

Optimal energy decay rates for Klein-Gordon equations with Kelvin-Voigt damping

Filippo Dell'Oro, Lassi Paunonen, David Seifert

A Klein-Gordon equation describes relativistic wave phenomena; Kelvin-Voigt damping adds a viscoelastic dissipation term. This paper proves that despite the generator having multiple eigenvalues on the imaginary axis—a technically awkward feature—all solutions' energy still decays to zero, and establishes the exact optimal polynomial decay rate for smooth initial data.

math.AP
Original abstract

We study the long-time behaviour of solutions to a one-dimensional linear Klein-Gordon equation with Kelvin-Voigt damping. One of the interesting features of the equation is that the generator of the associated $C_0$-semigroup has multiple spectral points on the imaginary axis. As our main result, we show that the energy of every possible solution converges to zero as time goes to infinity and, moreover, we provide an optimal polynomial energy decay rate for a certain class of solutions.

An inverse problem for semilinear wave equations on metric tree graphs

Sergei Avdonin, Matti Lassas, Jinpeng Lu et al.

Can you reconstruct an unknown network's structure and equation parameters just from boundary measurements? This paper shows that for a semilinear wave equation on a metric tree graph — a tree whose edges are actual intervals — the Dirichlet-to-Neumann map (recording how boundary inputs relate to boundary outputs) uniquely determines the graph's connectivity, edge lengths, potential, and nonlinear coefficient. It's a strong identifiability result for network-based inverse problems.

math.AP
Original abstract

We study the inverse problem for a semilinear wave equation on metric tree graphs. From the Dirichlet-to-Neumann map defined at all but one of the boundary vertices, we recover unknown connectivity of the graph, lengths of the edges, the time-independent potential and the time-dependent coefficient of the nonlinear term of the equation.

A Liouville theorem for ancient solutions of the parabolic Monge-Ampère equation with periodic data

Kui Yan, Jiguang Bao

The parabolic Monge-Ampère equation couples time evolution to a fully nonlinear spatial operator (the determinant of the Hessian), and ancient solutions are those defined for all past time. This paper proves any well-behaved ancient solution with periodic forcing must split into a linear time drift, a quadratic spatial part, and a fully periodic remainder—a Liouville-type rigidity theorem extending classical elliptic results to the parabolic setting.

math.AP
Original abstract

This article is concerned with the parabolic Monge-Ampère equation $-u_t\det D_x^2u=f$, where $f=f_1(x)f_2(t)$ and $f_1,f_2$ are positive periodic functions. We prove that any classical parabolically convex ancient solution $u$ must be of the form $-τt+p(x)+v(x,t)$, where $τ$ is a positive constant, $p(x)$ is a convex quadratic polynomial, and $v$ inherits both the spatial and temporal periodicity from $f$. This work extends previous contributions by Caffarelli-Li \cite{cl04} on periodic frameworks for the elliptic Monge-Ampère equations, and generalizes Zhang-Bao \cite{zb18}'s Liouville theorem for $f_2\equiv1$ in parabolic case.

Boosted Ground States for a Pseudo-Relativistic Schrödinger Equation with a double power nonlinearity

Pietro d'Avenia, Alessio Pomponio, Gaetano Siciliano et al.

Can a quantum wave packet travel at near-light speed without falling apart? This paper constructs 'boosted ground states'—stable, traveling wave solutions to a pseudo-relativistic Schrödinger equation (one incorporating special relativity via a square-root operator) with two competing nonlinear terms. Using constrained optimization, the authors prove these solutions exist for any speed below light and analyze how they behave as mass vanishes or speed approaches its limit, extending known results for simpler single-power equations.

math.AP
Original abstract

In this paper, we investigate the existence and limit behaviours of travelling solitary waves of the form $ψ(t,x)=e^{iλt}\varphi\left(x-vt\right)$ to the nonlinear pseudo-relativistic Schrödinger equation \[ i\partial_t ψ=(\sqrt{-Δ+m^2})ψ- |ψ|^{\frac{2}{N}}ψ-μ|ψ|^{q}ψ~~\text{ on }\mathbb{R}^N, \] for $m\ge 0$ and $|v|<1$. To this end, we introduce and analyse an associated constrained variational problem, whose minimizers are termed boosted ground states and the parameter $λ$ is obtained as a Lagrangian multiplier. We first provide a complete classification for the existence and nonexistence of such boosted ground states. Based on this classification, we then study several limiting profiles, for which the exact blow-up rate is also established.

Eventual regularity of the volume-preserving mean curvature flow in three and two dimensions

Vedansh Arya, Seongmin Jeon, Vesa Julin

Volume-preserving mean curvature flow reshapes a surface so its area decreases while enclosing fixed volume—think of a wobbly blob rounding into a sphere. Prior work showed convergence to a sphere in Hausdorff distance; this paper proves something stronger: after finite time the evolving surface becomes perfectly smooth and satisfies the flow equation classically, with exponential convergence in all Sobolev norms. The result holds in two and three dimensions and resolves the question of eventual regularity for this geometric evolution.

math.AP
Original abstract

The recent work of Morini-Oronzio-Spadaro and the third author shows that, in three dimensions, a flat-flow solution of the volume-preserving mean curvature flow that converges to a single ball, which is the case for instance when the initial perimeter is less than that of two disjoint balls, converges exponentially fast in Hausdorff distance. In this paper we strengthen this result by proving that after a finite time the flow becomes smooth, satisfies the equation in the classical sense and converges exponentially fast to the limiting ball in every C^k-norm. In the proof we develop a version of Brakke's epsilon regularity theorem adapted to our setting and derive the necessary nonlinear PDE estimates directly at the level of the discrete minimizing-movement scheme. The same result holds in the planar case.

Local well-posedness of spatially quasiperiodic gravity water waves in two dimensions

Mihaela Ifrim, Jon Wilkening, Xinyu Zhao

Can water waves have spatially quasiperiodic patterns — structured but never exactly repeating — and still evolve predictably? This paper provides the first proof that gravity water wave equations are locally well-posed (solutions exist, are unique, and depend continuously on initial data) for quasiperiodic initial conditions. The result opens the door to studying long-time behavior of realistic ocean waves beyond periodic models.

math.AP
Original abstract

We provide the first proof of local well-posedness for the two-dimensional gravity water wave equations with spatially quasi-periodic initial conditions. We represent the solution using holomorphic coordinates, which are equivalent to a conformal mapping formulation of the equations of motion. This allows us to compute the Dirichlet-Neumann operator via the Hilbert transform, which has a simple form in the spatially quasiperiodic setting. We use a Littlewood-Paley decomposition adapted to the quasiperiodic setting and establish multiplicative and commutator estimates in this framework.
