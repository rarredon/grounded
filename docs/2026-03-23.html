Irreducibility and Monodromy of Automorphic Galois Representations of $\mathrm{GL}(4)$

Alireza Shavali

Automorphic Galois representations are number-theoretic objects encoding symmetries of solutions to polynomial equations via the representation theory of GL(4). This paper proves these representations are irreducible over totally real fields for non-self-dual cases, and computes their monodromy groups — capturing how the representations vary — in both self-dual and non-self-dual settings, establishing strong 'big image' results that rule out unexpected hidden structure.

math.NT
Original abstract

We prove that over totally real fields, the $p$-adic Galois representations attached to non-self-dual regular algebraic cuspidal automorphic representations of $\mathrm{GL}(4)$ are irreducible. We then develop the theory of extra-twists in a general setting and use it to compute the monodromy group (over $\mathbb{Q}$) of these Galois representations, in both self-dual and non-self-dual settings, and prove $p$-adic and residual big image results.

Minimal Weierstrass models and regular models of hyperelliptic curves

Qing Liu

Hyperelliptic curves (generalizations of elliptic curves to higher genus) can often be written in multiple 'minimal Weierstrass forms' — the simplest polynomial equations defining them over a local field. This paper analyzes what happens when more than one such minimal model exists, characterizing stable reduction (a measure of how badly a curve degenerates) for genus-2 curves and computing key arithmetic invariants. The results clarify subtle structure in the arithmetic geometry of these curves.

math.NT
Original abstract

Let $C$ be a hyperelliptic curve of genus $g\ge 2$ over a discrete valuation field $K$ with perfect residue field. We study the minimal Weierstrass models of $C$. When there is more than one such model, we find interesting properties on the minimal regular model and the canonical model of $C$. For curves of genus $2$, we characterize the existence of the stable reduction in terms of the minimal Weierstrass models. When there is more than one such model, we can compute the Euler factor of $\mathrm{Jac}(C)$ and a volume form of the Néron model of $\mathrm{Jac}(C)$, using two specific minimal Weierstrass models.

Mahler's method and Carlitz logarithm

Guillaume Estienne

Carlitz logarithms are function-field analogues of classical logarithms over finite fields, and Papanikolas proved in 2007 that their linear independence implies algebraic independence — a striking result. This paper gives a new, more elementary proof using Mahler's method (analyzing functions satisfying functional equations under iteration), bypassing the heavy machinery of t-motives and extending earlier partial results by Denis.

math.NT
Original abstract

In 2007, Papanikolas established that if Carlitz logarithms of algebraic functions are linearly independent over the rational function field, then they are algebraically independent. The purpose of the present paper is to provide a new proof of this theorem using Mahler s method instead of the theory of t-motives. We revisit and extend the approach developed by Denis, which enabled him in 2006 to prove this result in the particular case of the logarithm of elements in Fq(theta) via a Mahler system.

Close fields, affine Springer fibers and fundamental lemmas

Sebastian Bartling, Kazuhiro Ito

A central challenge in number theory is proving 'fundamental lemmas'—identities between integrals that underpin the Langlands program—across different number systems. This paper shows that geometric objects called affine Springer fibers behave consistently across 'close local fields' (fields that share arithmetic structure), allowing two major fundamental lemmas to transfer from characteristic zero to positive characteristic. This resolves a longstanding technical barrier in automorphic forms.

math.NT math.AG
Original abstract

We prove a geometric local constancy theorem for affine Springer fibers in families of close local fields. Consequently, stable orbital integrals are locally constant in these families, and both the base change fundamental lemma and the standard endoscopic fundamental lemma transfer from characteristic zero to arbitrary positive characteristic.

Diophantine approximation with integers having no large prime factors

Kunjakanan Nath, Habibur Rahaman

Can irrational numbers be approximated unusually well by fractions whose numerators have no large prime factors? This paper shows that for any irrational α, infinitely many 'smooth' numbers n (numbers whose prime factors are all small) satisfy the approximation ‖nα‖ < n^{−θ} for explicit exponents θ. The result pushes Diophantine approximation into the smooth-numbers setting, combining a powerful technique called the dispersion method with estimates for Kloosterman sums.

math.NT
Original abstract

Given any irrational number $α$ and a real number $κ>0$, we show that for any $θ<θ_0(y)$, there are infinitely many $y$-smooth (friable) numbers $n$ such that $$\|nα\|<n^{-θ},$$ where $θ_0(y)=6/17$ if $\exp((\log\log n)^{2+κ})\leq y\leq n^{o(1)}$ and $θ_0(y)=20/59$ if $n^{o(1)}<y\leq n$. Here $\|x\|$ denotes the distance from $x$ to the nearest integer. Our proof is based on the dispersion method together with arithmetic inputs coming from the average bounds for Kloosterman sums over smooth numbers.

Fields with no everywhere good abelian varieties

Armand Brumer, Kenneth Kramer

Some number fields — algebraic extensions of the rationals — are so 'arithmetically constrained' that no nontrivial abelian variety (a higher-dimensional generalization of an elliptic curve) can have good reduction at every prime. This paper extends classical methods of Fontaine, Abrashkin, and Schoof to identify such fields systematically, finding nearly 25,000 examples up to degree 16 under the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis.

math.NT
Original abstract

We extend methods of Fontaine, Abrashkin and Schoof to obtain criteria determining number fields K over which no non-zero abelian variety with everywhere good reduction exists. As an application, under the GRH, we find 24744 such fields of various degrees up to 16.

Decidability of singularities in the Ekedahl--Oort stratification

Jean-Stefan Koskivirta, Lorenzo La Porta

Shimura varieties are geometric objects central to modern number theory, and their mod-p reductions are stratified by the Ekedahl–Oort stratification, which tracks how p-torsion of abelian varieties degenerates. This paper gives an algorithm to detect codimension-one singularities in these strata, characterizing regularity via the Frobenius action on the root datum. This makes previously intractable geometric questions about these moduli spaces computationally decidable.

math.NT math.AG
Original abstract

For an abelian type Shimura variety and an odd prime $p$ of good reduction, we characterize the regularity in codimension one of Zariski closures of Ekedahl--Oort strata in terms of the Frobenius action on the root datum. We give an algorithm that detects codimension one singularities for arbitrary Ekedahl--Oort strata. When the Shimura datum is of split type, we relate the singularities of Ekedahl--Oort strata to a stack of $G$-zips over the complex numbers. We study the existence of generalized Hasse invariants on this stack.

Fractional parts of powers of negative rationals

Qing Lu, Weizhe Zheng

Where do the fractional parts of ξ·(-p/q)^n land on the circle [0,1)? This paper proves they can't all cluster in a short interval — specifically, they must spread out over a length of at least (1 + q/p - q²/p²)/p. This sharpens our understanding of distribution of exponential sequences with negative bases, a subtler problem than the positive-base case due to sign alternation.

math.NT
Original abstract

We prove that for any real number $ξ\neq 0$ and any coprime integers $p>q\ge1$ such that $ξ$ is irrational or $q>1$, the image in $\mathbb{R}/\mathbb{Z}$ of the sequence $(ξ(-p/q)^n)_{n\ge 0}$ is not contained in any interval of length less than $(1+q/p-q^2/p^2)/p$.

On the central derivatives of l-functions and modularity of heenger cycles

Tuoping Du, Zhifeng Peng

How do L-functions — analytic objects encoding deep arithmetic information — relate to geometric data on algebraic curves? This paper proves that for general cusp forms (periodic functions central to number theory), the derivative of an L-function at its central point equals a height pairing between higher Heegner cycles, extending a celebrated formula of Gross-Zagier-Zhang. The result advances our understanding of the BSD conjecture and provides new modularity results for generating series built from these geometric cycles.

math.NT
Original abstract

This paper establishes an arithmetic intersection formula for central L-derivatives in higher weights.We prove that for a general cusp form (extending the previous result for newforms), the derivative is represented by the global height pairing between higher Heegner cycles. This result provides a framework for the Gross-Zagier-Zhang formula and its generalizations.Furthermore, we investigate the modularity of the generating series of Heegner cycles,proving a weak version of the conjecture and reducing the full modularity to a vanishing conjecture,for which we provide supporting evidence.

Infinite families of APN permutations in constrained trivariate classes over $\mathbb{F}_{2^m}$

Daniele Bartoli, Pantelimon Stanica

Almost Perfect Nonlinear (APN) functions over binary fields are critical in cryptography for maximizing resistance to differential attacks. This paper constructs infinite families of APN permutations—a rare and sought-after object—by extending known examples to trivariate polynomial maps over fields of odd degree, characterizing exactly when they permute via a clean algebraic root condition.

math.NT cs.CR cs.IT
Original abstract

We study trivariate permutation polynomials over $\mathbb{F}_{2^{m}}$ extending two APN permutation families of Li--Kaleyski (IEEE Trans. Inform. Theory, 2024) by allowing the scalar parameter to vary over $\mathbb{F}_{2^m}^*$. For \[ G_a(x,y,z)=(x^{q+1}+ax^qz+yz^q,\; x^qz+y^{q+1},\; xy^q+ay^qz+z^{q+1}), \] where $a\in\mathbb{F}_{2^m}^*$, $q=2^i$, $\gcd(i,m)=1$, and $m$ is odd, we prove that $G_a$ is a permutation if and only if an associated univariate polynomial has no root in $\mathbb{F}_{2^m}^*$, and that this condition is also equivalent to $G_a$ being APN. Hence, writing $d=q^2+q+1$, at least \[ \frac{2^m+1-(d-1)(d-2)2^{m/2}-d}{d} \] values of $a$ yield APN permutations $G_a$. In the binary case $q=2$, we show that $a=1$ is good whenever $7\nmid m$, recovering the Li--Kaleyski family. For the second family \[ H_a(x,y,z)=(x^{q+1}+axy^q+yz^q,\; xy^q+z^{q+1},\; x^qz+y^{q+1}+ay^qz), \] we obtain the same root criterion and prove that its defining polynomial is root-equivalent to that of $G_a$. Thus the same parameters $a$ give APN permutations in both families. We also prove strong inequivalence results. First, $G_a$ (resp.\ $H_a$) is diagonally equivalent to $G_1$ (resp.\ $H_1$) if and only if $a^{q^2+q+1}=1$; moreover, for $m>4$, $m\neq 6$, and $7\nmid m$, diagonal non-equivalence implies CCZ non-equivalence by the monomial restriction theorem of Shi et al.\ (DCC, 2025). In particular, when $q=2$ and $7\nmid m$, every good $a\neq 1$ gives APN permutations CCZ-inequivalent to Li--Kaleyski. Second, for the same range of $m$, no $G_a$ is CCZ-equivalent to any $H_b$. Hence these constructions yield two genuinely new, mutually inequivalent families of APN permutations on $\mathbb{F}_{2^{3m}}$.

The Dimension Conjecture Implies The Jacobi Bound Conjecture

Taylor Dupuy, David Zureick-Brown

The Jacobi bound conjecture predicts that a system of differential polynomial equations has solutions of order at most the permanent of a certain matrix built from the equations' degrees. This paper shows that another open conjecture—the Dimension Conjecture about differential algebraic varieties—would imply the Jacobi bound, linking two major unsolved problems in differential algebra.

math.AG math.AC
Original abstract

We prove that the Dimension Conjecture implies the Jacobi Bound Conjecture.

Taylor Dupuy, David Zureick-Brown

The Jacobi bound conjecture limits how complex the solutions of differential polynomial systems can be. This paper proves a strong version of that conjecture for 'generically reduced' components of differential schemes—roughly, solution sets with no hidden multiplicity at generic points—pushing the conjecture's resolution significantly forward for a broad geometric class.

math.AG math.AC
Original abstract

We prove the Strong Jacobi Bound Conjecture for generically reduced components of differential schemes.

Affineness of the maximal étale locus

Ivan Zelich

The purity of the ramification locus is a classical algebraic geometry theorem saying that where a map 'branches' has nice structure. This paper proves a stronger version: the maximal étale locus—the largest open set where a map is locally an isomorphism—is always affine. The proof uses a technical Tor-independence result proved via 'tilting,' a technique borrowing structure from characteristic-p algebra.

math.AG
Original abstract

In this paper we will prove a strong version of the celebrated purity of the ramification locus theorem in algebraic geometry. Our key input is a Tor-independence result for global sections of étale schemes over excellent regular local rings, which we will prove by tilting to perfect rings.

Quantum cohomology and irrationality of Gushel-Mukai fourfolds

Vladimiro Benedetti, Laurent Manivel, Nicolas Perrin

Gushel-Mukai fourfolds are a class of algebraic varieties whose rationality — whether they're geometrically equivalent to four-dimensional projective space — has been a major open question. By computing their quantum cohomology (a deformation of ordinary cohomology encoding curve-counting data), this paper proves that very general Gushel-Mukai fourfolds are irrational, and that any rational example must share the rational cohomology of a K3 surface.

math.AG
Original abstract

We compute the small quantum cohomology of Gushel-Mukai fourfolds. Following [13], our computations imply that the very general ones are not rational. Following [8], and thanks to a suitable deformation of the small quantum cohomology ring, we also deduce that a rational Gushel-Mukai fourfold has the same rational cohomology as some K3 surface.

On uniform large genus asymptotics of Witten's intersection numbers

Jindong Guo, Di Yang, Don Zagier

Witten's intersection numbers are quantities computed on the moduli space of algebraic curves — the space parameterizing all curves of a given topology — and control solutions to equations in mathematical physics like the Painlevé I equation. This paper derives sharp uniform asymptotic formulas for these numbers as the genus (complexity of the curve) grows large, and gives a new proof of a polynomiality conjecture about their asymptotic expansions. The uniformity makes the estimates applicable across all insertion types simultaneously.

math-ph math.AG math.CO nlin.SI
Original abstract

Following ideas from [14], we give a uniform large genus asymptotics for primitive psi-class intersection numbers on the moduli space of stable algebraic curves, and extend this result including insertions of zeros in a certain uniform way. Application to a particular formal solution of the Painlevé I equation is given. We also use a method from [14] to give a new proof of the polynomiality conjecture on large genus asymptotic expansions of psi-class intersection numbers.

Quantitative Stability for Minkowski's problem

Károly Böröczky, João Miguel Machado, João P. G. Ramos

Minkowski's problem asks: given a measure describing how a convex body's surface is oriented, can you recover the body? This paper proves a quantitative stability result — if two such measures are close, their corresponding bodies are close too — with an explicit bound involving the Fraenkel asymmetry (how far a set is from being a ball) and a natural distance between measures. This extends classical existence theory to a robust, quantitative framework for both classical and Lp-Minkowski bodies.

math.AP math.MG math.OC
Original abstract

We derive quantitative stability results for Minkowski bodies, as well as their counterparts, the $L_p$-Minkowski bodies in the range $1 \le p \neq n$. We prove that, for every pair of probability measures $μ,ν$ satisfying a quantitative form of the classical dispersion assumptions yielding existence of such bodies, we have a control of the form \[ α(E_μ, E_ν)^2 \le C \mathrm{d}_{\mathrm{C}}(μ,ν)^{1 + \frac{1}{n}}, \] where $α$ denotes the Fraenkel asymmetry and $\mathrm{d}_{\mathrm{C}}$ is the dual-convex distance of probability measures on the sphere. Our arguments are based on a variational problem whose optimizers are Minkowski bodies, for which we can obtain strong-concavity properties with the quantitative Brunn-Minkowski and isoperimetric inequalities.

Asymptotic linear stability of columnar vortices driven by Coriolis force

Shuang Miao, Siqi Ren, Zhifei Zhang

Do rotating columnar vortices—tornado-like structures sustained by the Coriolis force—remain stable when perturbed? This paper proves asymptotic linear stability for a class of such vortices in 3D fluid flow, meaning small disturbances decay over time. The key innovation is constructing a 'distorted Fourier basis' tailored to the problem's linearized operator, a broadly applicable technique representing a crucial stepping stone toward full nonlinear stability.

math.AP
Original abstract

In this paper, we establish the asymptotic linear stability of a class of Coriolis-driven columnar vortices for the 3-D axisymmetric Euler equations. This result represents a critical step toward proving the nonlinear asymptotic stability of such vortices. The key and widely applicable strategy is to construct a distorted Fourier basis, which is achieved by solving a two-parameter $(c, ξ)$-dependent Schrödinger equation associated with the linearized operator of the system. To capture the precise asymptotic behavior of the solution, we decompose the $c-ξ$ plane into distinct regions, with the partitioning guided by the leading-order profiles of the Schrödinger equation across different parameter regimes.

The one-dimensional compressible Navier-Stokes equations in critical regularity spaces

Raphaël Danchin

The compressible Navier-Stokes equations describe fluid flow where density can vary. Global well-posedness—guaranteeing solutions exist and behave stably forever—was established in multiple dimensions decades ago, but the 1D case in 'critical regularity' (the sharpest functional space where the problem is scale-invariant) remained open. This paper closes that gap, revealing that 1D requires genuinely new techniques because the critical spaces behave differently than in higher dimensions.

math.AP
Original abstract

We are concerned with the barotropic compressible Navier-Stokes equations on the real line. Our primary goal is to establish the global well-posedness in a critical regularity framework in the case where the initial data are small perturbations of a stable constant state. Surprisingly, even though the result in the multi-dimensional case is by now classical, the one-dimensional case has not been elucidated yet as far as we know. This is due to the fact that in the critical framework, the regularity of the velocity is so negative that some nonlinear terms are out of control. Here, we overcome the difficulty by considering the equations in the mass Lagrangian coordinates system. Granted with a global well-posedness statement, we then establish optimal time decay estimates and investigate the high viscosity limit, pointing out the convergence of the specific volume to the solution of some ordinary differential equation, after time and space rescaling.

Lipschitz solutions to mean field games with a major player and applications

Charles Meynard

Mean field games model strategic interactions among vast numbers of agents; adding a 'major player' whose actions significantly influence everyone else complicates the governing 'master equation' system. This paper establishes a notion of weak (Lipschitz) solutions requiring only Lipschitz-continuous coefficients, proves uniqueness of smooth solutions, and confirms consistency with Nash equilibria—making the theory applicable to far rougher, more realistic settings.

math.AP math.OC math.PR
Original abstract

This paper introduces a notion of weak solution for the coupled system of master equations in mean field games with a major player. It extends the previously introduced notion of Lipschitz solutions in mean field games. By relying on a probabilistic representation of the system of master equations, we prove that there can exist at most one sufficiently smooth solution and that it is consistent with the associated Nash equilibrium. In this approach, coefficients are only required to be Lipschitz, in particular, no differentiability assumption with respect to probability measures is needed. In a second part, we apply this notion of solution to prove the existence and uniqueness of solutions to MFGs with a major player on intervals of arbitrary length. Our argument relies on assuming that the intensity of the Brownian common noise driving the state of the major player is sufficiently large, as well as a joint displacement monotonicity assumption between the coefficients of minor players and those of the major player. Most notably, this joint monotonicity allows us to prove that the threshold of volatility can be taken independently of the horizon of the game considered, without any long time decoupling assumption on the dependence between minor players and the major player. Finally, inspired by recent extragradient methods for mean field games, we present an algorithm that converges exponentially fast to the solution of the major-minor probabilistic system under this monotonicity assumption. Thanks to the generality of our approach, all results presented in this article hold for mean field games of controls with a major player.

Lipschitz regularity of solutions to two-phase $p$-Laplacian free boundary problems with right hand side

Fausto Ferrari, Claudia Lederman

Two-phase free boundary problems model interfaces—like melting fronts—where a solution transitions between two regimes, governed here by the p-Laplacian operator. The paper proves that viscosity solutions (a broad solution concept requiring no classical differentiability) are locally Lipschitz continuous, meaning they can't change faster than a linear rate—the best regularity one can hope for. This result is new even in the simplest homogeneous case and works for all p between 1 and infinity.

math.AP
Original abstract

We prove the local Lipschitz continuity of viscosity solutions for two-phase free boundary problems for the $p$-Laplacian with non-zero right hand side, where $p\in (1,\infty)$. This is the optimal regularity for the problem. We also obtain the local Hölder continuity for a larger class of problems. The results introduced here are new even in the homogeneous situation, that is, when the right hand side is zero. Our work applies to merely viscosity solutions, which allows a wide applicability.

Two-phase free boundary problems for operators with nonstandard growth

Fausto Ferrari, Monica Jacob, Claudia Lederman

This survey paper examines two-phase free boundary problems—equations modeling sharp interfaces between two materials—governed by PDEs with 'nonstandard growth,' meaning the operator's behavior varies with position rather than following a fixed power law. It highlights recent results and the mathematical challenges posed by this variable-growth setting, motivated by applications in physics and materials science.

math.AP
Original abstract

In this paper we focus the attention on free boundary problems ruled by partial differential equations with nonstandard growth, presenting in particular some recent results. The interest in these problems stems from the diverse applications that motivate their study and from the challenging mathematical difficulties they pose.

Smooth finite time singularity formation without quantization

Istvan Kadar

The energy-critical wave equation can develop singularities in finite time via 'bubbling,' where energy concentrates into shrinking solitons; previous constructions required soliton sizes to follow a rigid quantization rule. This paper constructs smooth approximate blowup solutions where multiple solitons shrink simultaneously at rate t^ν along different directions, with freely chosen sizes and signs—breaking the quantization constraint. The result uses geometric singular analysis to give a cleaner, more flexible framework for understanding wave equation blowup.

math.AP
Original abstract

We revisit the finite time singularity formation of Krieger-Schlag-Tataru [KST09] for the focusing energy critical wave equation in $\mathbb{R}^{3+1}$ from a geometric singular-analytic point of view, following Hintz [Hintz23]. We construct $C^{ν/2-}$ regular approximate solutions that settle down to multiple solitons, shrinking at a rate $t^ν$ with $ν>1$, and approaching the origin on different geodesics $\{x=zt\}$. By fine tuning the velocities, sizes and signs of the solitons, we are able to construct smooth ansätze with any $ν>8$. Using robust energy estimates, the ansätze are corrected to exact solutions.

Local Existence and Finite-Time Singularity Formation in the Vlasov-Poisson-Isotropic Landau System

Jin Woo Jang, Junsung Kim

The Vlasov-Poisson-isotropic Landau system models plasma physics by coupling particle distribution, self-generated electric fields, and collisions. This paper establishes that solutions exist locally in time for small initial data, then proves solutions can blow up in finite time — meaning physical quantities become infinite. This is significant because finite-time singularity formation in kinetic collision models is poorly understood, and this system provides a tractable setting to study it rigorously.

math.AP
Original abstract

The isotropic Landau (Coulomb) operator was introduced in kinetic theory by Krieger and Strain (Comm. Partial Differential Equations, 2012). In this work, we study the spatially inhomogeneous Vlasov--Poisson--isotropic Landau system. We first establish a local--in--time existence theory for the Cauchy problem: for initial data satisfying a suitable smallness condition in an appropriate norm, there exists a non--negative solution on a time interval $[0,T]$, where the lifespan $T$ depends on the size of the initial data. Beyond the local theory, we investigate a mechanism that may lead to the breakdown of global existence. We show that finite--time singularity formation can occur in the gravitationally attractive case, provided that the weak solution satisfies certain a priori regularity and decay assumptions, the initial gravitational field energy exceeds the kinetic energy, and the resulting energy gap dominates the diffusive effect of the collision operator. As a consequence, if the solution is further assumed to belong to a suitable measure space up to the maximal existence time, it collapses to a single point in physical space at that time. The proof of finite--time singularity formation is based on deriving an upper bound for the second spatial moment, which becomes negative in finite time.

On the $(k+2,k)$-problem of Brown, Erdős and Sós for even integers $k$

Yan Wang, Jiasheng Zeng

The Brown–Erdős–Sós conjecture asks how many edges a 3-uniform hypergraph (where edges are triples of vertices) can have if every k edges together touch more than k+2 vertices. This paper confirms the conjecture for all even integers k, establishing that the edge density scales like n² with an explicit constant. This resolves half of a 50-year-old open problem in extremal combinatorics using powerful probabilistic and algebraic tools.

math.CO
Original abstract

Let $f^{(r)}(n;s,k)$ denote the maximum number of edges in an $r$-graph on $n$ vertices in which every $k$ edges span more than $s$ vertices. Brown, Erdős and Sós in 1973 conjectured that for every $k\geq 2$, the limit $\lim_{n\to\infty} n^{-2} f^{(3)}(n;k+2,k)$ exists and verified the conjecture for $k=2$ by showing that $\lim_{n\to\infty} n^{-2} f^{(3)}(n;4,2)=\frac{1}{6}$. Delcourt and Postle, building on the work of Glock, Joos, Kim, Kühn, Lichev and Pikhurko, proved that for every $k\geq 2$, the limit $\lim_{n\to\infty} n^{-2} f^{(3)}(n;k+2,k)$ exists, thereby solving this conjecture. Their approach was later generalised by Shangguan to every uniformity $r\geq 4$: the limit $\lim_{n\to\infty} n^{-2} f^{(r)}(n; rk-2k+2,k)$ exists for all $r\geq 3$ and $k\geq 2$. However, its exact value was not determined. When $k\in\{2,3,\ldots,7\}$, the exact values of $\lim_{n\to\infty} n^{-2} f^{(r)}(n; rk-2k+2,k)$ were determined by Glock, Joos, Kim, Kühn, Lichev, Pikhurko, Rödl and Sun. Very recently, the limit for $k=8$ and $r\geq 4$ was determined by Pikhurko and Sun. For a general even integer $k$, Letzter and Sgueglia obtained the exact values of $\lim_{n\to\infty} n^{-2} f^{(r)}(n;rk-2k+2,k)$ for every even integer $k$ and uniformity $r\geq 2+\sqrt{2}\,k^{3/2}$. In this paper, we determine the exact value of $\lim_{n\to\infty} n^{-2} f^{(r)}(n;rk-2k+2,k)$ for every even integer $k\geq 4$ and $r\geq 2+\sqrt{\frac{3}{2}k-4}$, and show that it is $\frac{1}{r^2-r}.$

On a maximal anti-Ramsey conjecture of Burr, Erdős, Graham, and Sós

Matija Bucic, Kaizhe Chen, Jie Ma

An edge-coloring of a graph is 'rainbow' for a subgraph H if every copy of H gets all distinct colors; the maximal anti-Ramsey function measures the minimum number of colors forcing this. Burr, Erdős, Graham, and Sós conjectured the exact threshold for odd cycles, and this paper confirms it for all odd cycles C_{2k+1} with k≥4, resolving a decades-old conjecture using Turán-type density arguments.

math.CO
Original abstract

Given a graph $H$, the maximal anti-Ramsey function $f(n,e,H)$ denotes the minimum integer $f$ for which there exists an $n$-vertex graph $G$ with at least $e$ edges admitting an edge-coloring with $f$ colors in which each copy of $H$ in $G$ is rainbow. In the late 1980s, Burr, Erdős, Graham, and Sós conjectured that for every odd cycle $C_{2k+1}$ with $k \ge 3$, $f(n, \lfloor n^2/4 \rfloor + 1, C_{2k+1}) = n^2/8 + o(n^2)$. In this note, we confirm this conjecture for all $k \ge 4$. More generally, we establish the asymptotic formula $$f\left(n,e,C_{2k+1}\right)=\frac{e}{2}+\frac{n}{2}\sqrt{e-\frac{n^2}{4}}+o(n^2),$$ for the entire non-trivial range of $\left\lfloor n^2/4 \right\rfloor+1\le e\le \binom{n}{2}$.

Improvement on the Erdős-Kleitman conjecture via the KKL theorem
