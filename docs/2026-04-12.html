A Liouville-Type Inequality for Values of Mahler M-Functions

Boris Adamczewski, Colin Faverjon

Mahler M-functions are power series satisfying functional equations of the form f(x^q) = rational function of f(x); their values at algebraic points are mysterious transcendental numbers. This paper proves a Liouville-type inequality—a lower bound on how well these values can be approximated by rationals—ruling out that any such value is a Liouville number (an extremely well-approximable transcendental). This resolves a longstanding open problem in transcendence theory.

math.NT
Original abstract

We establish a Liouville-type inequality for the values, at a common nonzero algebraic point, of arbitrary Mahler Mq-functions. As an application, we prove that no such value is a Liouville number, or even a U -number. This solves a long-standing problem in the field.

Universal sums of generalized polygonal numbers of almost prime length

Soumyarup Banerjee, Ben Kane, Kwan To Ng

Polygonal numbers (like triangular or square numbers) have generalizations, and a sum formula is 'universal' if it represents every positive integer. This paper studies which sums of generalized polygonal numbers are universal when inputs are restricted to 'almost primes' (numbers with few prime factors), proving finiteness theorems that reduce universality to checking finitely many cases — making the problem computationally tractable.

math.NT
Original abstract

In this paper, we consider universal sums of generalized polygonal numbers. Fixing $m\in\mathbb{N}_{\geq 3}$, we show two finiteness theorems for universal sums of generalized polygonal numbers whose inputs have a restricted number $L$ of prime divisors (counting multiplicity) away from an finite set of exceptional primes. In the first theorem, we fix $m$ and uniformly bound the finite check independent of $L\geq 900$, and in the second theorem, we give an optimal bound for the finiteness check if $L$ is larger than a constant times $\log(m)$.

On Lower Bounds for sums of Fourier Coefficients of Twist-Inequivalent Newforms

Moni Kumari, Prabhat Kumar Mishra, Jyotirmoy Sengupta

Given two modular forms f and g that aren't twists of each other (i.e., not related by multiplying by a Dirichlet character), this paper establishes that the sums of their Fourier coefficients at primes can't be 'too smooth' — specifically, the largest prime factor of a_f(p)+a_g(p) grows at least like (log p)^{1/14} for almost all primes p. This quantifies how arithmetically complex these coefficient sums must be, connecting modular form theory to prime factorization structure.

math.NT
Original abstract

In this article, we address the lower bounds for the sums $a_f(p)+a_g(p)$ of the $p$-th Fourier coefficients of two twist-inequivalent, non-CM normalized newforms $f$ and $g$. Our main result shows that for such forms with integer Fourier coefficients, the largest prime factor of $a_f(p)+a_g(p)$ satisfies $P(a_f(p)+a_g(p)) > (\log p)^{1/14} (\log \log p)^{3/7-ε}$ for almost all primes $p$ and for any $ε> 0$. Beyond primes, we apply Brun's sieve to show that a similar phenomenon holds for a set of positive integers with natural density one. The main result is further strengthened under the Generalized Riemann Hypothesis, where we establish exponential growth for the absolute value of $a_f(p)+a_g(p)$ in terms of $p$.Additionally, we derive an interesting result related to the multiplicity one theorem, demonstrating that if the sum $a_f(p)+a_g(p)$ is small for a positive-density subset of primes, then $f$ and $g$ must be twist-equivalent by a quadratic character.

$p$-adic Theory for Partial Toric Exponential Sums

C. Douglas Haessig

Wan previously proved that partial toric L-functions — generating functions encoding exponential sums over subsets of algebraic tori — are rational, using ℓ-adic (geometric) methods. This paper reproves rationality via p-adic analysis in the spirit of Dwork, expressing partial L-functions as alternating products of 'twisted Fredholm determinants,' operators whose determinants aren't automatically well-behaved, requiring new techniques to establish convergence and rationality.

math.NT
Original abstract

Wan proved the rationality of partial toric $L$-functions using $\ell$-adic techniques. In this paper, we present a $p$-adic proof in the spirit of Dwork. We demonstrate that partial $L$-functions can be expressed as an alternating product of twisted Fredholm determinants. These twisted determinants appear to be intrinsic to the analytic structure of partial $L$-functions, and unlike their classical counterparts, twisted Fredholm determinants of completely continuous operators are not automatically $p$-adic entire functions. However, for partial $L$-functions they will be $p$-adic meromorphic. After proving rationality, we construct a $p$-adic cohomology theory and give a $p$-adic cohomological formula for partial toric $L$-functions. Last, we show they have a unique $p$-adic unit root which may be explicitly written in terms of $A$-hypergeometric series.

On a conjecture of Deines

Mentzelos Melistas

Two elliptic curves over the rationals are called discriminant twins if they share the same minimal discriminant and conductor—key numerical invariants—without being isogenous (essentially the same curve). Since 2014, Deines conjectured infinitely many such pairs exist among semi-stable curves. This paper delivers an explicit infinite family, fully proving the conjecture and settling a decade-old open problem in arithmetic geometry.

math.NT
Original abstract

Two elliptic curves defined over $\mathbb{Q}$ are called discriminant twins if they have the same minimal discriminant and the same conductor. Deines, in 2014, conjectured that there exist infinitely many semi-stable non-isogenous discriminant twins. In this article we present an explicit infinite family of semi-stable non-isogenous discriminant twins, providing a proof for Deines' conjecture.

On the cohomology of negative Tate twists via cyclotomic descent

Taewan Kim, Seunghun Ryu

Galois cohomology measures how symmetry groups of number fields interact with arithmetic objects; Tate twists are standard ways to shift these objects. This paper shows that cohomology for all negative Tate twists is governed by a single 'universal' complex over the cyclotomic tower, with each twist recoverable via Iwasawa theory—a framework using p-adic power series—by plugging in one value. This unifies and simplifies computations central to modern algebraic number theory.

math.NT
Original abstract

We show that the Galois cohomology of negative Tate twists can be organized by a single universal cyclotomic complex over the cyclotomic tower of $\mathbb{Q}$. Using cyclotomic descent and Teichmüller branch decomposition, we prove that a negative twist contributes only on the corresponding branch and is recovered by specializing the Iwasawa variable at a single point; equivalently, it is computed as the fiber of $γ-u^{-m}$, or $T=u^{-m}-1$ in Iwasawa coordinates. In the case $\mathbb{Q}_p/\mathbb{Z}_p$, this gives explicit descriptions of $H^1$ and $H^2$ in terms of the quotient and torsion of the $S$-ramified Iwasawa module.

Weighted averages of arithmetic functions and applications to equidistribution

Vitaly Bergelson, Michael Reilly, Florian K. Richter

How do weighted averages of arithmetic functions behave when the weights grow in structured ways? This paper gives general estimates for such averages when the underlying function follows a Gaussian distribution pattern — covering classical functions like the number of prime factors. The results yield new equidistribution theorems, meaning these functions spread evenly across residue classes in a precise sense, with applications to analytic number theory.

math.NT
Original abstract

For a wide range of functions $W\colon\mathbb{N}\to\mathbb{N}$, we establish a general result for estimating weighted averages of the form \[ \mathbb{E}^{W}_{n \le N} f(\vartheta(n))= \frac{1}{W(N)}\sum_{n=1}^N (W(n)-W(n-1))f(\vartheta(n)), \] where $f\colon \{1,\ldots,N\}\to\mathbb{C}$ is an arbitrary function, and $\vartheta(n)$ is any arithmetic function that adheres to a certain Gaussian distribution condition. (In particular, one can take $\vartheta(n)=Ω(n)$, $\vartheta(n)=ω(n)$, or $\vartheta(n)=Ω(q_n)$, where $Ω(n)$ and $ω(n)$ count the number of prime factors of $n$ with and without multiplicities respectively, and $q_n$ denotes the $n$-th squarefree number.) As an application of our main theorem, we show that if $h(n)$ is a function from a Hardy field with polynomial growth then $(h(\vartheta(n)))_{n\in\mathbb{N}}$ is uniformly distributed mod $1$ if and only if one of the following (mutually exclusive) conditions is satisfied: (i) $\lim_{x\to\infty} \frac{|h(x)-p(x)|}{x \log x}=\infty$ for all $p(x)\in \mathbb{Q}[x]$; (ii) $\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{|h(x)-p(x)|}{\sqrt{x}}=\infty$ for each $p(x)\in \mathbb{Q}[x]$ and there exists $q(x)\in \mathbb{Q}[x]$ such that $\lim_{x\to\infty}\frac{|h(x)-q(x)|}{x}<\infty$. This leads to novel applications regarding the uniform distribution of sequences of the from $h(Ω(n))$, $h(ω(n))$, and $h(Ω(q_n))$. For example, we show that $(Ω(n)^c)_{n\in\mathbb{N}}$ is uniformly distributed mod $1$ if and only if $c$ is a non-integer greater than $\frac{1}{2}$.

Random conic bundle surfaces satisfy the Hasse principle

Christopher Frei, Efthymios Sofos

The Hasse principle asks: if an equation has solutions over the real numbers and over all modular arithmetic systems, must it have a rational solution? This paper proves the answer is yes for 100% of conic bundle surfaces (algebraic surfaces built from families of conics) over the rational projective line, in a precise density sense. This is a major quantitative result in arithmetic geometry, where failures of the Hasse principle are common but shown here to be vanishingly rare.

math.NT math.AG
Original abstract

We establish the Hasse principle for $100\%$ of conic bundles over $\mathbb{P}^1_{\mathbb{Q}}$.

Arithmetic intersections on non-split Cartan modular curves

Jonathan Love, Elie Studnia, Jan Vonk

CM divisors are special points on modular curves associated to imaginary quadratic fields, and their height pairings measure arithmetic intersection in a precise sense. Gross–Kohnen–Zagier computed these pairings on split Cartan curves; this paper handles the harder 'non-split' case, determining intersection numbers on X_ns⁺(p) at all finite primes. The key challenge—bad reduction at p—is resolved through careful local analysis, extending a classical result to the remaining case.

math.NT
Original abstract

Let $p$ be a prime number, and let $Δ_1,Δ_2 < 0$ be two coprime fundamental discriminants. When $p$ splits in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{Δ_1})$ and $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{Δ_2})$ the height pairings of the corresponding CM divisors on $X_{\mathrm{spl}}^+(p)$ were determined by Gross--Kohnen--Zagier [GKZ87]. When $p$ is inert, we determine the arithmetic intersection numbers of the corresponding divisors on $X_{\mathrm{ns}}^+(p)$ at all finite primes. The key point of our analysis is at the prime of bad reduction $p$: to determine the intersection numbers at $p$, we provide a moduli interpretation for the smooth locus in the regular model of $X_{\mathrm{ns}}^+(p)$ over $\mathrm{Spec}(\mathbb{Z})$ constructed by Edixhoven--Parent [EP24].

Boundary Cohomology of Sp6(Z): Trivial Representation

Ryuto Mitoma

The symplectic group Sp₆(ℤ) is an important arithmetic group—a discrete symmetry group with deep ties to number theory and modular forms. This paper explicitly computes its boundary cohomology (topological invariants associated with the 'boundary at infinity' of the associated symmetric space) with trivial coefficients, using the Borel-Serre compactification and a spectral sequence. Such computations are valuable inputs for understanding automorphic forms and the broader Langlands program.

math.NT math.AG

In this article, we compute the boundary cohomology of the arithmetic group Sp6(Z) with coefficients in the trivial representation. Our computation utilizes the Borel-Serre compactification and the associated spectral sequence.

On Iwasawa theory of abelian varieties over $\mathbb{Z}_p^2$-extension with applications to Diophantine stability and integally Diophantine extensions

Meng Fai Lim

Iwasawa theory studies how arithmetic objects like elliptic curves behave across infinite towers of number fields. This paper extends that theory to abelian varieties over two-dimensional p-adic extensions and applies the results to Diophantine stability — asking whether a variety having a rational point in one field forces rational points in related fields. Along the way it refines known cases of Mazur's conjecture on how the size of certain groups grows in these towers.

math.NT
Original abstract

We present certain results on the Iwasawa theory of an abelian variety with potentially good ordinary reduction at all primes above $p$. These are then applied to study Diophantine stability and integally Diophantine extensions. Along the way, we also obtain some results pertaining to Mazur growth conjecture which refine previous results of Gajek-Leonard, Hatley, Kundu and Lei. Finally, we extend our investigation to the case of an elliptic curve with good supersingular reduction at the prime $p$ and make a similar analysis.

Small gaps between consecutive zeros of the Riemann zeta-function

Shōta Inoue

The zeros of the Riemann zeta-function are conjectured to never cluster too closely together, and the parameter μ measures the smallest possible normalized gap. The longstanding barrier near μ < 0.515 had resisted improvement for years. This paper introduces a 'resonance-correlation method' combining two classical techniques and proves μ < 0.50895 under the Riemann Hypothesis, meaningfully pushing toward the conjectured value of 0.5.

math.NT
Original abstract

In this paper, we introduce the resonance-correlation method to study small gaps between consecutive zeros of the Riemann zeta-function. Our method is based on a synthesis of Montgomery's pair correlation approach and the Montgomery-Odlyzko method. As an application, we break the persistent practical barrier around $0.515$ and prove $μ< 0.50895$ under the Riemann Hypothesis.

On the computation of base-change lifts and lifts of Hida families

Iván Blanco-Chacón, Luis Dieulefait, Antti Haavikko

A base-change lift takes a classical modular form and produces a Hilbert modular form over a larger number field, with predictable relationships between their Hecke eigenvalues (arithmetic data encoding prime behavior). The paper gives explicit eigenvalue formulas, factorizes the associated L-functions over characters of abelian fields, and extends the construction to Hida families — p-adic interpolations of modular forms — advancing the toolkit for studying automorphic forms via base change.

math.NT
Original abstract

We derive an explicit formula for the Hecke eigenvalues of a Hilbert modular form which is a base-change lift of a classical newform to a totally real Galois number field. We show that for a totally real abelian number field $F$ the $L$-function of a base-change lifted form can be factorized as a product of twisted $L$-functions over the characters of $F$. Moreover, we use the formula for the Hecke eigenvalues of a base-change lift to prove the existence of a base-change lift of a Hida family. In particular, we show that a Hida family of classical Hecke eigenforms can be lifted to a formal power series that specializes to the base-change lifts of the Hida family of classical cusp forms.

Partial sums of random multiplicative functions with supercritical divisor twists

Jad Hamdan

A Steinhaus random multiplicative function assigns independent random complex values on primes, and the α-divisor function counts generalized divisors; together they create a 'twisted' random sum with unusually heavy-tailed behavior when α ∈ (1,2). The paper establishes precise moment estimates matching predictions from supercritical Gaussian multiplicative chaos — a probabilistic model for extreme fluctuations — extending Harper's landmark results to this more singular regime.

math.NT math.FA math.PR
Original abstract

Let $f$ be a Steinhaus random multiplicative function, and for $α\in \mathbb{R}$, let $d_α$ denote the $α$-divisor function. For $α\in (1,2)$ we establish that $$ \mathbb{E}\bigg\{\Big|\frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}\sum_{n\leq x} d_α(n)f(n)\Big|^{2q}\bigg\} \ll \frac{(\log x)^{2q(α-1)}}{(\log\log x)^{3αq/2}(1-αq)+1} $$ uniformly for $q\in [0,1/α]$ and all large $x$. This matches predictions from the theory of supercritical Gaussian multiplicative chaos, and provides an analogue of a seminal result of Harper corresponding to the critical ($α=1$) case. Our approach is based on studying the measure of level sets of an Euler product associated with $f$, and yields a short proof of Harper's upper bound at $α=1$ (implying Helson's conjecture at $q=1/2$). As an additional application, we obtain a conjecturally sharp bound for the pseudomoments of the Riemann zeta function in a certain parameter range, showing that $$ \lim_{T\to\infty}\frac{1}{T}\int_T^{2T} \bigg|\sum_{n\leq x}\frac{d_α(n)}{n^{1/2+it}}\bigg|^{2q} \mathrm{d}t \ll \frac{(\log x)^{2q(α-1)}}{(\log\log x)^{3αq/2}}, $$ for $α\in (1,2)$ and small $q>0$. This answers a question of Gerspach.

There are infinitely many Hilbert cubes of dimension 3 in the set of squares

Andrew Bremner, Christian Elsholtz, Maciej Ulas

A Hilbert cube of dimension d is a set of 2^d integers formed by choosing subsets of d fixed values and adding them to a base. The paper proves there are infinitely many 3-dimensional Hilbert cubes lying entirely within the perfect squares, answering a question of Brown, Erdős, and Freedman about whether such structures in the squares have a bounded maximum dimension.

math.NT math.CO
Original abstract

A Hilbert cube of dimension $d$ is the set of integers \[ H(a_{0}; a_{1}, \ldots, a_{d})=a_{0}+\{0, a_{1}\}+\cdots+\{0, a_{d}\}=\left\{a_{0}+\sum_{i=1}^{d}\varepsilon_{i}a_{i}:\;\varepsilon_{i}\in\{0,1\}\right\}. \] Brown, Erdős and Freedman asked whether the maximal dimension of a Hilbert cube in the set $\cal{S}=\{n^2:\;n\in\mathbb{N}\}$ of integer squares is absolutely bounded or not. Dietmann and Elsholtz proved that if $H(a_{0}; a_{1}, \ldots, a_{d})\subset \cal{S}\cap [0, N]$, then $d\leq 7 \log\log N$ for all sufficiently large values of $N$. Here we prove that there exist at least $\gg N^{1/8}$ Hilbert cubes $H(a_{0}; a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3})$ with $a_{0}, a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3}\in [0,N]$ in the set of squares. Moreover, we prove that for each $i, j\in\{0, 1, 2, 3\}$ with $i<j$, the set $$ \left\{\frac{a_{i}}{a_{j}}:\;H(a_{0}; a_{1}, a_{2}, a_{3})\subset S\right\} $$ is dense in the set of positive real numbers (in the Euclidean topology).

Partition congruences are divisibility patterns in the number of ways integers can be written as sums—Ramanujan famously discovered several for ordinary partitions. This paper proves an infinite family of congruences modulo 4 and 8 for restricted two-color partitions, resolving a conjecture of Banerjee, Bringmann, and Bachraoui using the theory of modular forms.

math.NT
Original abstract

Recently, Andrews and Bachraoui investigated congruences for certain restricted two-color partitions. They made two conjectures for Ramanujan type congruences and a vanishing identity for the limiting sequence. Very recently, Banerjee, Bringmann and Bachraoui confirmed these three conjectures by relating the corresponding generating function to modular forms and mock theta functions. Extensions of the Carlitz-McConnel and Blokhuis-Sziklai theorems for unions of cyclotomic classes

Maosheng Xiong, Chi Hoi Yip

When can a function on a finite field be forced into a rigid, predictable form? The classical Carlitz-McConnel theorem says: if ratios of differences always land in a proper multiplicative subgroup, the function must be a linear map twisted by a power of p. This paper extends that result to unions of cosets—more flexible sets—under mild conditions, broadening our toolkit for classifying highly structured functions over finite fields.

math.NT math.CO
Original abstract

Let $p$ be a prime, let $q=p^n$, and let $D\subseteq \mathbb{F}_q^\ast$. A celebrated result of Carlitz and McConnel states that if $D$ is a proper subgroup of $\mathbb{F}_q^\ast$, and $f:\mathbb{F}_q\to\mathbb{F}_q$ is a function such that $(f(x)-f(y))/(x-y)\in D$ for all $x\neq y$, then $f$ must be of the form $f(x)=ax^{p^j}+b$. In this paper, we extend their result to the setting where $D$ is a union of cosets of a fixed subgroup of $\mathbb{F}_q^\ast$, under a mild assumption. In a similar spirit, we also investigate maximum cliques in related Cayley graphs over finite fields, strengthening several results of Blokhuis, Sziklai, and Asgarli and Yip.

The Domb Ap'ery-limit and a proof of the Ramanujan Machine conjecture Z2

Alex Shvets

The Ramanujan Machine project uses algorithms to auto-conjecture formulas involving fundamental constants like ζ(3) (the sum of reciprocal cubes). This paper proves one such conjecture, Z₂ = 12/(7ζ(3)), by showing a precise limiting ratio of two combinatorial sequences called Apéry-like numbers and Domb numbers, using machinery from the theory of modular forms.

math.NT
Original abstract

We prove that the ratio $B_n/D_n$ of the Apéry-like sequence $B_n$ to the Domb numbers $D_n$ converges to $(7/24)ζ(3)$, and that $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 64^n/(n^3 D_n D_{n-1}) = (56/3)ζ(3)$. Order drop, Hecke descent, and a mod $p^4$ supercongruence for symmetric-cube hypergeometric coefficients

Alex Shvets

A supercongruence says two quantities agree modulo a high power of a prime—here, p⁴—which is far stronger than ordinary divisibility. This paper proves such a result for a sequence built from hypergeometric functions (power-series generalizing classical functions), using modular curves and symbolic algebra to reduce an apparently analytic statement to structured arithmetic.

math.NT
Original abstract

We prove that the symmetric-cube coefficients $A_n = (-27)^n [z^n] {}_2F_1(1/3,1/3;1;z)^3$ satisfy the supercongruence $A_{mp} \equiv A_m \pmod{p^4}$ for every prime $p \geq 5$ and every positive integer $m$. A foliation partitions a geometric space into lower-dimensional leaves, and a rank-1 log canonical foliation is a particularly well-behaved one-dimensional version on a singular algebraic variety. Margarida Mendes Lopes, Rita Pardini, Roberto Pignatelli

A theta characteristic on a curve is a square root of its canonical bundle, and each carries a parity (even or odd) that is topologically stable in families. A one-step counterexample to the normalized Nash blowup conjecture

Alvaro Liendo, Ana Julisa Palomino, Gonzalo Rodríguez

The normalized Nash blowup conjecture predicted that repeatedly 'blowing up' singularities of an algebraic variety — replacing singular points with limit data from nearby smooth points — would eventually resolve them. This paper constructs an explicit 5-dimensional counterexample in characteristic three where one blowup step reproduces the original singularity, disproving the conjecture. Combined with prior work, counterexamples now exist in every dimension ≥5 and every field characteristic.

math.AG
Original abstract

We construct an explicit normal singular affine toric variety X of dimension five over an algebraically closed field of characteristic three such that the normalized Nash blowup of X already contains an open affine subset isomorphic to X. Combined with previously known examples, this yields one-step counterexamples in every dimension greater than or equal to five and every characteristic. The characteristic-three case is the most delicate: the previously known counterexample in dimension four requires a two-step iteration of the normalized Nash blowup, and our example demonstrates that in dimension five and higher the minimal number of iterations needed to produce a loop is one.

Differential graded categories in holomorphic symplectic geometry

Borislav Mladenov

Holomorphic symplectic manifolds are complex geometric spaces with a rich symmetry structure central to modern mathematical physics. This paper studies several 'differential graded categories'—algebraic structures organizing geometric objects called Lagrangian D-branes on these spaces—and proves they satisfy a strong simplification property called formality. Formality means the category's higher algebraic complexity collapses to something much simpler, making these structures far more tractable for applications in mirror symmetry and quantization.
